# Frontend Engineer — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Frontend as a Strategic Discipline** — How the frontend role evolves; connecting craft to business outcomes
2. **Design System Architecture Decision** — Leading creation/adoption across multiple teams; governance, versioning, adoption
3. **Frontend Platform / Architecture Decision** — Choosing SSR vs. CSR vs. ISR; monolith vs. micro-frontends; rendering strategy for entire product line
4. **Accessibility Strategy at Scale** — Org-wide a11y standards; building into CI/CD; making WCAG compliance part of Definition of Done
5. **Legacy Migration Leadership** — jQuery/Angular to React migration; strangler pattern; managing team morale during long refactors
6. **Technical Debt Management** — Creating UI tech debt register; prioritizing high-impact fixes; refactoring alongside feature delivery
7. **Mentoring Frontend Developers** — Coaching through CSS architecture, component patterns, accessibility; building frontend guild
8. **Cross-Team Stakeholder Alignment** — Aligning product, design, engineering on frontend standards; organizational politics around tech choices
9. **Developer Experience (DX) Leadership** — Improving build times, CI/CD for frontend, monorepo standards, unified tooling
10. **Business Impact Through Frontend** — Connecting UI decisions to revenue, conversion, engagement; presenting to leadership in business terms

## Phase Structure
- **Strategic opening (5-7 min):** "How do you think about the frontend role evolving?" / "Philosophy on balancing DX with UX?" — tests organizational thinking
- **Deep leadership narratives (25-30 min):** 2-3 stories with "Table of Contents" structure (overview then deep dive); multi-month arcs, multiple teams
- **Impact and reflection (5-8 min):** "What was the biggest frontend bet that didn't pan out?"

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Behavioral round **determines your level** at senior/staff — not a checkbox, it's the calibration mechanism
- Stories must show impact across **four dimensions**: technical (architecture), people (mentoring/conflict), process (standards/CI/CD), strategic/business (roadmap/stakeholders)
- Unique to senior frontend: **design system governance** (10+ teams adopting shared components), **rendering strategy decisions** affecting product suites, **accessibility as organizational capability**
- Must discuss **business metrics** influenced through frontend decisions (conversion, page speed → revenue, a11y → new markets)
- Staff-level must show they were a **multiplier** for other developers
- Time horizon: **many months**, not days or weeks

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Individual coding accomplishments or bug fixes (too tactical)
- Single technology choice without organizational context
- Generic "leadership" questions that could apply to any role
- Not probing for **failed initiatives**
- Accepting "we migrated to React" without probing incremental plan, team morale, business case

## Probe Patterns
- Architecture decisions: "Who disagreed? Their argument? How resolved? What differently now?" (intellectual honesty + stakeholder navigation)
- Mentoring: "Specific growth area? How did you adapt coaching approach? How long?" (real mentorship vs. one-off reviews)
- Migration: "Give one-sentence summary of each phase" (executive-level communication)
- Design system: "How did you handle the team that didn't want to adopt? Governance model?" (organizational influence)
- Business impact: "Can you connect that frontend decision to a specific business metric?"
