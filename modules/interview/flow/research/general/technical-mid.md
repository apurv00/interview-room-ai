# General / Any Role — Technical Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **System thinking** and component interaction
2. **Architecture awareness** (HLD + LLD)
3. **Technical decision-making** and trade-off analysis
4. **API design** and schema considerations
5. **Performance and scalability** reasoning
6. **Code review philosophy** and quality standards
7. **Debugging complex** multi-component issues
8. **Technical mentoring** approach

## Phase Structure
- Phone screen (45-60 min) → System design round → Coding round → Behavioral
- Must cover both high-level (HLD) and low-level design (LLD)
- Own features end-to-end: API design, schemas, scale, integration

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Key question: "Can they build end-to-end?"
- Must reason about **scalability and performance**, not just correctness
- Drive architectural discussions, make and justify design decisions with trade-offs
- Every decision needs explicit trade-off discussion (e.g., NoSQL sacrifices consistency for availability)

## Anti-Patterns
- Knowing tools but not why you'd choose one over another
- Inability to discuss trade-offs
- Designing without considering scale or real-world constraints
- Not connecting technical decisions to business requirements
- Over-engineering when simple approaches suffice
