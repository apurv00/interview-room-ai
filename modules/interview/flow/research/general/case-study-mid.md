# General / Any Role — Case Study Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Multi-stakeholder problem analysis** (RACI, stakeholder mapping)
2. **Hypothesis-driven investigation** with issue trees
3. **Scenario analysis** and sensitivity testing
4. **Advanced frameworks** (SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, value chain)
5. **Data synthesis** across multiple sources
6. **Strategic recommendation** with implementation roadmap
7. **Risk assessment** and mitigation planning
8. **Persuasive communication** of findings

## Phase Structure
- Partner-led interviews with senior interviewers (12+ years)
- Deep drilling into experiences and independent problem-handling
- Multi-round: first round tests structure, second tests personality and challenge handling
- May involve group case exercises

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Shift from "can you structure" to "can you handle conflicting stakeholder demands independently"
- 80/20 thinking: identifying vital few drivers
- Must synthesize, not just analyze — connect data into coherent narrative
- Benchmarking against industry standards and root cause analysis
- Implementation feasibility is part of the recommendation

## Anti-Patterns
- Relying on memorized frameworks (experienced interviewers spot this)
- Focusing too narrowly (costs only, ignoring revenue)
- Not incorporating interviewer data into reasoning
- Analysis paralysis without converging on recommendation
- Presenting without clear "so what" and next steps
