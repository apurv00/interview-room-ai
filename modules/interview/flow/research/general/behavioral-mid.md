# General / Any Role — Behavioral Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Cross-functional leadership** and influence without authority
2. **Ownership of mistakes** and accountability
3. **Giving/receiving difficult feedback**
4. **Driving projects through ambiguity**
5. **Mentoring and developing others**
6. **Navigating organizational politics**
7. **Handling competing priorities** from multiple stakeholders
8. **Recovering from project failure**

## Phase Structure
- Deeper probing: 3-4 questions explored in depth with follow-ups rather than breadth
- Interviewers drill into specifics, testing consistency of narrative

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Shift from "can you work with others" to "can you lead without formal authority"
- Must earn trust, take ownership, process failure, grow from it
- Cross-functional collaboration is central — influencing outside reporting line
- STAR answers must show measurable organizational impact, not just personal growth

## Anti-Patterns
- Claiming credit for team outcomes without specifying personal role
- Inability to articulate lessons from failure
- Generic leadership platitudes instead of concrete examples
- Framing all conflict as "other person's fault"
- Not quantifying impact
