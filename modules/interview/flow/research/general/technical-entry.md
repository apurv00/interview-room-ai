# General / Any Role — Technical Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Problem-solving approach** and logical reasoning
2. **Fundamental CS concepts** (data structures, algorithms basics)
3. **Learning ability** and technical curiosity
4. **Basic debugging** and troubleshooting methodology
5. **Software development lifecycle** understanding
6. **Communication of technical concepts**
7. **Tool/technology awareness** (not mastery)

## Phase Structure
- Online assessment (60-90 min) → Phone screen (45 min) → Onsite 2-3 coding rounds
- Focus on potential over expertise; interviewers drive the conversation
- Expect explanations of approach as much as correct answers

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Tests **foundational reasoning and ability to learn**, not deep expertise
- Problem-solving process matters more than the answer
- Must break problems into smaller steps and explain logical reasoning
- General aptitude tests may supplement technical questions
- Showing how you'd figure it out is the point

## Anti-Patterns
- Memorizing answers without understanding
- Inability to explain thought process while solving
- Freezing on unfamiliar problems instead of reasoning through
- Claiming expertise in barely-used technologies
- Not asking clarifying questions before diving in
