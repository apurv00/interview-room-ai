# General / Any Role — System Design Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Business requirements to technical architecture** translation
2. **Large-scale distributed system** design (millions of users)
3. **Cross-cutting concerns** (security, privacy, compliance, observability)
4. **Organizational alignment** with architecture (Conway's Law)
5. **Failure mode analysis** and disaster recovery
6. **Technology evaluation** and migration strategy
7. **Multi-team system ownership** and API contracts
8. **Cost optimization** at scale

## Phase Structure
- Candidate fully drives; interviewer observes strategic thinking
- 60 min, complex open-ended problem with evolving requirements
- Proactively address non-functional requirements without prompting
- May include "architecture review" of past systems

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Must proactively address cross-cutting concerns (operational toil, security, privacy, team hiring) without prompting
- Align architecture with organizational reality — Conway's Law awareness
- Scope problems and limit blast radius of inevitable failures
- Shift from "how to build it" to "how to make it succeed in a real organization"
- Demonstrated ability to lead major design efforts, mentor others, influence at org level

## Anti-Patterns
- "Theoretically optimal" system without organizational constraints
- Not proactively addressing failure modes and recovery
- Ignoring cost implications
- Treating as coding exercise rather than strategic discussion
- Not considering team boundaries and ownership
- Missing security/privacy/compliance
