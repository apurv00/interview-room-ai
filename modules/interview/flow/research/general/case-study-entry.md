# General / Any Role — Case Study Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Structured problem decomposition** (MECE principle)
2. **Basic framework application** (profitability, market sizing)
3. **Quantitative reasoning** and estimation
4. **Hypothesis formation** and testing
5. **Data interpretation** and insight extraction
6. **Logical communication** of recommendations
7. **Handling ambiguity**

## Phase Structure
- Prompt (2 min) → Clarifying questions (3 min) → Framework (5 min) → Analysis (20 min) → Recommendation (5 min)
- Common types: market sizing, basic profitability, simple market entry
- Interviewer provides significant guidance and data prompts

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Tests **structured thinking**, not business expertise
- MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) is foundational skill
- Must build frameworks from scratch, not memorize templates
- Clear logic and organizing thoughts under pressure
- Simple arithmetic and estimation essential

## Anti-Patterns
- Memorizing frameworks and applying rigidly without adapting
- Jumping to solutions before understanding
- Ignoring interviewer-provided data
- Over-engineering structure, leaving too little time for insights
- Not doing basic math correctly under pressure
