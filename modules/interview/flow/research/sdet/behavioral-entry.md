# SDET / QA Engineer — Behavioral Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **First critical bug discovery** — finding, reporting, communicating impact
2. **Developer collaboration** — joint debugging, evidence-based bug reports
3. **Speaking up about quality** — first push-back when something wasn't ready
4. **Learning testing approaches** — systematic thinking about breaking software
5. **Handling conflicting information** — ambiguous requirements, contradictory acceptance criteria
6. **Flaky test encounters** — first unreliable test diagnosis
7. **Balancing thoroughness with deadlines** — triage under time pressure
8. **Teamwork under pressure** — contributing to a release on tight timelines

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (2 min):** Motivation for QA/testing
- **Core STAR stories (15-20 min):** 2-3 scenarios on bug finding, collaboration, quality advocacy
- **Reflection (3-5 min):** Quality mindset evolution

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Candidates reference bootcamps, internships, or first-job experiences
- Differentiator is a **quality mindset** — thinking about edge cases naturally, caring about UX beyond requirements
- May not have dramatic org-level stories but should show **analytical thinking and willingness to speak up** as a junior
- SDET-specific: natural instinct to ask "what could go wrong?" before "does it work?"

## Anti-Patterns
- Cannot name a specific bug they found
- Describes QA as "just following test cases"
- Blames developers with no collaborative framing
- No interest in automation
- Cannot distinguish testing from quality

## Probe Patterns
- "Walk me through exactly how you found that bug — what made you look there?"
- "How did the developer respond? What would you do differently now?"
- "When you had limited time, what was your framework for choosing what to test?"
