# SDET / QA Engineer — Behavioral Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Test strategy overhaul** — leading major change in automation approach
2. **Flaky test suite resolution** — systematic fix that unblocked deployments
3. **Quality advocacy under pressure** — convincing teams to delay for quality
4. **Cross-team QA influence** — standards/practices adopted by other teams
5. **Automation ROI decisions** — data-backed case for what to automate vs. not
6. **Test infrastructure ownership** — tooling/pipeline improvements that boosted velocity
7. **Disagreement resolution** — conflict with developers about test failures or scope
8. **Mentoring junior testers** — teaching approaches, code review for test code

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (2 min):** QA philosophy evolution
- **Core STAR stories (18-22 min):** 3-4 scenarios emphasizing ownership
- **Depth probes (5-8 min):** Systemic fixes, metrics to justify investments

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Shift from *executing* tests to **owning test strategy**
- Judged on how they think about risk, encode it in automation, handle flaky systems
- Key differentiator: **influence without authority** — getting teams to invest in quality without being a manager
- Interviewers look for candidates who **prevent bugs**, not just report them

## Anti-Patterns
- Describes themselves as "the person who runs tests" rather than shaping strategy
- No example of influencing a team
- Focuses on technical details without business impact
- Rigid "automate everything" thinking
- No evidence of mentoring

## Probe Patterns
- "What prompted the strategy overhaul — incident or gradual realization?"
- "How did you measure whether the new approach was better?"
- "When the team pushed back, what evidence did you use?"
- "What resistance did you face implementing the framework?"
