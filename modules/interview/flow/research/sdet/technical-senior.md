# SDET / QA Engineer — Technical Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Test platform architecture** — infrastructure serving multiple teams with different stacks
2. **Quality engineering at scale** — shared libraries, internal testing SDKs, self-service test environments
3. **Test infrastructure as a product** — product management rigor: users, roadmaps, adoption metrics
4. **Advanced CI/CD quality gates** — canary testing, feature flag validation, blue-green, rollback triggers
5. **Performance engineering** — load testing pipelines, capacity planning, SLO-based gates, APM integration
6. **Chaos engineering and resilience testing** — fault injection, game days, distributed failure mode testing
7. **Visual regression and AI-assisted testing** — screenshot comparison at scale, ML test generation, self-healing locators
8. **Quality metrics and reporting** — defect escape rate, MTTD, test effectiveness ratio, leadership dashboards
9. **Shift-left strategy** — developer testing culture, TDD, code review for testability, quality as engineering default
10. **Emerging practices** — contract testing for microservices, observability-driven testing, testing in production, AI/ML testing

## Phase Structure
- **Architecture discussion (10-15 min):** Design test platform for a large org
- **Strategy and metrics (8-12 min):** Quality metrics, test effectiveness, leadership reporting
- **Advanced topics (8-10 min):** Chaos, performance, AI-assisted testing
- **Leadership lens (5-8 min):** Mentoring, standards, adoption strategy

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Assesses **architectural ownership and strategic quality engineering**
- Designs test *platforms*, not just frameworks
- Test infrastructure as a product with users (engineering teams), roadmaps, adoption strategies
- Key differentiators: **scaling across org, defining quality SLOs, integrating testing into engineering platform**
- Must have opinions on industry direction (AI testing, testing in production, observability-driven testing)
- Scaling requirements (browsers, devices, tech stacks) and infrastructure to support them are central

## Anti-Patterns
- Designs without flaky test management or maintenance strategy
- Cannot explain test data at scale
- Ignores CI/CD integration
- No parallelization/scaling awareness
- Cannot connect metrics to business outcomes
- Only single-team framework experience
- No mentoring or standards-setting evidence

## Probe Patterns
- "How handle teams that don't want to use the shared platform?"
- "How measure test effectiveness — not just coverage, but catching real bugs?"
- "Design for 5 teams to 50 teams — what changes?"
- "How get buy-in for chaos engineering? Manage experiment risk?"
- "Quality metrics dashboard — what does it show, how does leadership use it?"
