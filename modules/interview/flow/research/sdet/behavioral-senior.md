# SDET / QA Engineer — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Quality culture transformation** — org-wide change in how quality is valued
2. **Executive influence** — convincing leadership to invest in test infrastructure over features
3. **Building QA teams** — hiring, structuring, mentoring quality engineers
4. **Testing standards at scale** — enforcing quality across multiple product lines
5. **Quality metrics and measurement** — defining metrics that drive improvement
6. **Shift-left advocacy** — changing engineering habits, moving quality earlier
7. **Crisis quality decisions** — high-stakes ship-or-hold calls under time pressure
8. **Cross-org quality alignment** — QA across teams with different stacks/cultures
9. **Strategic technical bets** — adopting contract testing, chaos engineering, AI-assisted testing

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (2 min):** Vision for QA, world-class quality culture
- **Core narratives (20-25 min):** 3-4 deep stories with organizational impact
- **Strategic reflection (5-8 min):** Quality measurement, where QA is headed

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Must demonstrate **organizational impact**, not just team-level improvements
- Describe building quality into culture: rituals (3 Amigos kickoffs, bug bashes, quality dashboards), workshops on testable stories, celebrating teams that reduce flake rates
- Making quality "part of default conversations, not an afterthought"
- Must translate **technical quality concerns into business language**
- SDET-specific: quality is a product, not a gatekeeping function

## Anti-Patterns
- Cannot describe improvements beyond immediate team
- Views QA as gatekeeping rather than enabling
- No examples of influencing non-engineering stakeholders
- Cannot articulate quality impact in business terms
- Builds QA as a silo
- No evidence of developing other leaders

## Probe Patterns
- "How handle resistance from teams who see quality investment as slowing them?"
- "ROI argument — how framed for leadership?"
- "What rituals changed how the org thinks about quality?"
- "Ship-or-hold decision — what was your framework?"
