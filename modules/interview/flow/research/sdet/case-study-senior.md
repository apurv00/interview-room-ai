# SDET / QA Engineer — Case Study Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Quality strategy for an entire organization** — first QA architect at a 200-person org
2. **Quality across teams with different maturity** — 15 teams, different stacks, different quality levels
3. **Production incident systemic fix** — incident cost $2M, design systemic prevention
4. **Test infrastructure as internal product** — build self-service platform for engineering teams
5. **Quality metrics program** — define metrics, dashboards, leadership reporting
6. **Shift-left transformation** — change engineering culture to embed quality earlier
7. **Build vs. buy for test tooling** — evaluate vendors vs. internal tools at scale

## Phase Structure
- **Problem framing (10 min):** Restate business challenge as quality opportunity. Map stakeholders.
- **Strategy design (15 min):** Phased approach (crawl-walk-run). Team structure. Success metrics.
- **Deep dive (15 min):** Interviewer picks thread — adoption resistance, metrics design, platform architecture
- **Organizational impact (10 min):** How get buy-in? How measure culture change? How handle teams that resist?

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Scenarios are **organizational**, not team-level — "you are the first QA architect" scope
- Must articulate quality in **business terms**: cost of defects, deployment confidence, engineering velocity impact
- Must address **people and process alongside technology** — quality culture transformation
- Expected to discuss **phased adoption** — not big-bang changes
- SDET-specific: quality is an engineering capability, not a separate function

## Common Problems
- "You're hired as first QA architect at a 200-person startup. 90-day plan?"
- "15 teams, 5 different stacks, no shared quality standards. Design the strategy."
- "Production outage cost $2M due to missed regression. Design systemic prevention."
- "Build self-service test infrastructure — some teams use React, others use mobile native."
- "Define quality metrics that leadership uses to make investment decisions."
- "Engineering culture is 'move fast, QA will catch it.' How do you change this?"

## Anti-Patterns
- Staying at tool/framework level instead of organizational strategy
- Big-bang transformation instead of phased adoption
- Quality as gatekeeping instead of enabling
- No business-language framing for quality investments
- Designing for one team instead of the organization
- No plan for measuring culture change success

## Probe Patterns
- "How handle the team that says 'we don't need QA, we write unit tests'?"
- "How justify the platform investment to the CFO?"
- "What does success look like after 6 months? After 18 months?"
- "How do you avoid the test platform becoming a bottleneck?"
- "What quality metrics would you put on the engineering leadership dashboard?"
