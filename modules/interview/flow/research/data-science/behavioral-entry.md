# Data Science — Behavioral Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **First data project walkthrough** — "Tell me about a data project you worked on. What was your role and outcome?"
2. **Communicating findings to non-technical stakeholders** — "Describe explaining complex technical findings to a non-technical audience"
3. **Data quality / messy data challenge** — "Tell me about a challenging data quality issue"
4. **Learning a new tool or technique** — "How you taught yourself a new tool under time pressure"
5. **Working on a cross-functional team** — "Describe a project with a cross-functional team"
6. **Handling ambiguity in analysis** — "Started an analysis without clear requirements"
7. **Receiving and incorporating feedback** — "Critical feedback on your analysis"
8. **Balancing multiple deadlines** — "How did you prioritize when multiple teams needed data?"

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (5 min):** Background, why data science, what excites you
- **Core stories (25-30 min):** 3-4 STAR questions about projects and communication
- **Wrap-up (5 min):** Questions for interviewer

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Accept **academic/bootcamp/personal projects** — no production experience required
- Heavy emphasis on **learning velocity and intellectual curiosity**
- Communication is the **#1 differentiator** — "even the best analysis falls flat if stakeholders can't act on it"
- Must describe projects in **layman's terms** without jargon
- DS-specific: probe whether you understand the *business impact* of your analysis, not just the technique

## Anti-Patterns
- **Kaggle-only syndrome:** Copying notebooks without understanding the process
- **ML tunnel vision:** Focusing on model-building, ignoring data cleaning, EDA, stakeholder context
- **No quantified outcomes:** "I built a model" vs "improved accuracy from X to Y"
- **Hypothetical framing:** "What I typically do..." instead of specific past examples
- **Overselling resume:** Claiming deep expertise in tools barely used

## Probe Patterns
- "Walk me through the data pipeline for that project" (did they actually do the work?)
- "What would you do differently?" (self-awareness)
- "How did you decide which visualization for that audience?" (communication intent)
- "Hardest part of cleaning that dataset?" (hands-on experience)
