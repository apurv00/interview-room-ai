# Data Science — Case Study Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a provided dataset
2. **Simple predictive modeling** (churn, classification, regression)
3. **Metric definition and basic product analytics**
4. **Take-home assignment with presentation**

## Phase Structure
- **Receive prompt (5 min):** Dataset + business question. Often take-home (48-72 hours) or 45-60 min live.
- **Clarify (5 min):** Define terms ("what counts as churn?"), confirm time frame, identify target variable.
- **Explore (15 min):** EDA — distributions, missing values, correlations. Summarize key patterns.
- **Model/Analyze (15 min):** Simple model (logistic regression, random forest) or segmentation. Explain feature choices.
- **Present findings (10 min):** Executive summary, 3-5 insights, actionable recommendations tied to business.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Heavy emphasis on **take-home assignments** rather than live case discussions
- Tests clean EDA and **presenting findings to non-technical audience**
- Model sophistication matters far less than **clarity of thought and business connection**
- Must state assumptions explicitly upfront

## Common Problems
- "Analyze sales data to identify top 3 reasons for customer churn" (Telco/SaaS datasets)
- "Predict which customers will convert to paid"
- "User engagement data — what's driving the WAU drop?"
- "Classify fraudulent transactions. Present approach and findings."
- "Forecast weekly sales for retail chain" (Walmart-style)

## Anti-Patterns
- Neural networks when logistic regression suffices
- Perfect analysis but missing the business "so what"
- Burying assumptions at the end
- Messy notebooks with no narrative
- 80% time on modeling, 20% on EDA (should be inverted at this level)
- Raw metrics without actionable recommendations

## Probe Patterns
- "Why this model over alternatives?"
- "What assumptions about this data?"
- "How explain this finding to a product manager?"
- "What differently with more time or data?"
- "Business impact if we act on your recommendation?"
