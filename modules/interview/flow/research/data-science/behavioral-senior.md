# Data Science — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Setting data strategy for team or org** — "How have you defined a DS roadmap? Aligned with business objectives?"
2. **Building or scaling a DS team** — "Hired and developed data scientists. What did you look for?"
3. **Influencing product direction through data** — "Data insights that fundamentally changed a product's direction"
4. **Driving organizational data culture** — "Getting non-data teams to adopt data-driven decision-making"
5. **Navigating executive disagreements** — "Push back on VP or C-level with data"
6. **Cross-functional leadership without authority** — "Led large initiative across engineering, product, data without direct reports"
7. **Technical vision and architecture decisions** — "Build vs buy vs partner for ML infrastructure"
8. **Handling ambiguity at organizational scale** — "Defining a problem space the company hadn't articulated"
9. **Mentoring senior ICs** — "Developing senior data scientists into leads or managers"
10. **Strategic data bet that didn't pay off** — "Major data initiative that failed"

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (5 min):** Scope of current role, org size, reporting structure
- **Core stories (35-40 min):** 4-5 deep questions testing strategic thinking, not execution
- **Wrap-up (5 min):** Vision — "Where do you see DS going at this company?"

## What Makes This Level Unique
- "Tests far more than technical skills — measuring ability to think strategically, communicate insights, and create measurable business impact"
- At Meta E6+/Staff: **2 behavioral + 2 leadership rounds vs 1 technical**
- Must demonstrate **influence without authority** — "having colleagues organically adopt your approaches is evidence of impact"
- Stories must link to **product metrics, retention, growth, efficiency** — "why a problem matters" > "how you solved it"
- Must show **systems thinking**: single analysis → team roadmap → org strategy → company goals
- Must articulate how they've **built culture**, not just shipped projects

## Anti-Patterns
- **Execution-focused only:** What you built without strategic "why" and organizational "so what"
- **IC framing at senior level:** "I did X" without "I enabled the team" or "I influenced the org"
- **No failures:** Strategic bets that didn't work — absence signals dishonesty or risk aversion
- **Confusing management with leadership:** Process management (standups) instead of vision-setting
- **Unable to articulate trade-offs:** Must show where NOT to invest and why

## Probe Patterns
- "How get buy-in from VP of Engineering?" (executive influence)
- "What did you intentionally NOT do, and why?" (strategic prioritization)
- "How measure whether data culture shift was working?" (meta-metrics)
- "Someone you mentored now in leadership role?" (multiplier effect)
- "Joined our org tomorrow — first 90 days?" (strategic vision)
