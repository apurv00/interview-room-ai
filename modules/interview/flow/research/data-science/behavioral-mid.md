# Data Science — Behavioral Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Influencing a stakeholder who disagreed with the data** — "Convince a non-technical stakeholder to adopt your data-driven recommendation"
2. **A/B testing ownership and decision-making** — "Ran an A/B test. Impact on product roadmap?"
3. **Conflicting metrics / KPI disagreements** — "Conflicting KPI definitions between teams"
4. **Model deployment with business trade-offs** — "Chose between accuracy and something else (speed, interpretability, fairness)"
5. **Cross-functional data influence** — "Your analysis changed a product or business decision"
6. **Working with incomplete data** — "Decision you made with incomplete data"
7. **Managing competing priorities** — "Multiple teams needed your support — how prioritize?"
8. **Project that failed** — "Data project that didn't go as planned"
9. **Mentoring teammates** — "Helped junior colleague grow their data skills"

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (5 min):** Current role, scope of impact, what you're looking for
- **Core stories (30-35 min):** 4-5 deep-dive questions probing *why* decisions were made
- **Wrap-up (5 min):** Questions showing product/business awareness

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Shift from "did you do the work" to **"did you drive impact and influence decisions"**
- Must own the **end-to-end analytical lifecycle**: problem framing → data collection → analysis → recommendation → follow-through
- DS-specific: Must demonstrate **product sense** — frame recommendations in revenue lift, cost savings, risk mitigation terms
- At FAANG, behavioral often **decides the final offer more than technical rounds**
- A/B testing fluency: not just running tests, but designing, interpreting nuanced results, making go/no-go calls
- Amazon-specific: LP stories require quantified business impact — "increased CTR from 2.1% to 3.4%, driving $800K quarterly revenue"

## Anti-Patterns
- **Technically correct but practically useless:** "Listing statistical tests when asked why customer activity dropped instead of asking about customer lifetime value"
- **No stakeholder context:** Analysis in isolation without who consumed it and what changed
- **Vague outcomes:** "We improved the metric" without specific numbers
- **Credit issues:** Either hoarding or unable to articulate specific contribution
- **Never mentioning failure:** Signals lack of self-awareness

## Probe Patterns
- "What metric did you choose and why that one over alternatives?" (product sense)
- "What did the stakeholder push back on? How address?" (influence depth)
- "How decide sample size for that A/B test?" (experimentation rigor)
- "What if test result was inconclusive?" (ambiguity tolerance)
- "Raw data to final recommendation — walk me through" (end-to-end ownership)
