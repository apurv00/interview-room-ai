# Product Manager — Case Study Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Design a Product from Scratch** — "Design a travel planning product for families" (Meta classic)
2. **Product Strategy** — "Should Spotify enter podcasting?"
3. **Metrics Deep Dive** — "PM for Facebook Events. Define success metrics and set goals."
4. **Improve + Monetize** — "Improve LinkedIn and increase revenue without hurting UX"
5. **Go-to-Market** — "Launching B2B analytics tool. Design GTM strategy."
6. **Competitive Response** — "Competitor launched free version of your paid feature. What do you do?"
7. **Product Tradeoff** — "Feature A (high impact, 6mo) or Features B+C (moderate, 2mo each). Walk through decision."
8. **Multi-Sided Platform** — "Design marketplace for local service providers" (balance supply/demand)
9. **Root Cause Analysis** — "Conversion rate dropped 15% this quarter. Diagnose and fix."

## Phase Structure
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Clarify & Scope | 2-3 min | Business context, company stage, constraints. Goals (growth vs. revenue vs. retention). |
| Market & Users | 3-4 min | Competitive landscape. Behavioral segmentation (not just demographics). |
| Problem Structuring | 4-5 min | Problem tree / opportunity map. 3-4 areas with sizing intuition. |
| Solution Design | 5-7 min | 2-3 solutions with detail: user flows, key screens, technical feasibility. |
| Prioritization | 3-4 min | RICE, ICE, or weighted scoring. Quantitative intuition. |
| Go-to-Market / Rollout | 2-3 min | Phased rollout. A/B test design. Launch-day vs. week-4 metrics. |
| Metrics & Guardrails | 3-4 min | North star + input metrics + guardrails. |
| Edge Cases & Risks | 2 min | Cannibalization, tech debt, regulatory, international. |

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Business context matters:** Ask "What's the company's strategic priority?" before designing.
- **Quantitative intuition:** "This could affect ~20% of our DAU" with rough reasoning.
- **Multi-stakeholder awareness:** Engineering cost, design feasibility, legal/privacy, business model.
- **Tradeoff articulation:** Best answer has clearest tradeoff analysis, not the "right" solution.
- **Metrics sophistication:** Guardrail metrics + leading vs. lagging indicators.
- **GTM is part of the case:** Mid-level expected to address how to launch, measure, iterate.

## Common Problems
- Design new product in adjacent space for known company
- Improve mature product's key metric by X%
- Respond to competitive or market shift
- Diagnose metric decline with structured decomposition
- Build/buy/partner recommendation

## Anti-Patterns
- Generic frameworks without adaptation to context
- Ignoring business model (no monetization path)
- Single-metric myopia (conversion without retention/brand)
- No competitive awareness
- Over-indexing on novelty when obvious solution exists
- Skipping rollout/experimentation strategy

## Probe Patterns
- "How different if optimizing revenue instead of growth?"
- "Engineering says 6 months. Get 80% of value in 6 weeks?"
- "What data would you look at before committing?"
- "How fits with company's broader portfolio?"
- "A/B shows +5% conversion but -2% retention. What do you do?"
- "Walk me through first 30 days after launch."
