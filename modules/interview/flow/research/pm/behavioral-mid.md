# Product Manager — Behavioral Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Product Launch Ownership** — "Tell me about a product you shipped end-to-end." Defining question. Full lifecycle: discovery to launch to measurement. Tradeoffs. Success metrics.
2. **Stakeholder Conflict & Influence Without Authority** — "Managing conflicting stakeholder requirements." Must understand each stakeholder's underlying motivation. Data to de-personalize conflict.
3. **Saying No / Prioritization Under Constraints** — "Tell me about killing a feature or saying no." Tests conviction, frameworks (impact, effort, strategic alignment), relationship maintenance.
4. **Data-Driven Decision Making** — "Using data to change product direction" / "Decision with incomplete data." Fluent about metrics (retention, conversion, NPS), A/B testing, balancing quant+qual.
5. **Cross-Functional Leadership** — "Leading cross-functional team through a challenge." Involves engineering, design, marketing, sales, and/or legal — not just engineering.
6. **User Advocacy & Empathy** — "Championing user perspective against business pressure." Tests genuine user obsession vs. metrics obsession.
7. **Handling Product Failure** — "A product or feature that failed." Tests accountability, early detection, stakeholder communication, process changes.
8. **Technical Collaboration** — "Working through a technical constraint threatening product vision." Demonstrates understanding tradeoffs without pretending to be an engineer.
9. **Metrics & Success Definition** — "How do you define success for a product?" Leading vs. lagging indicators, counter-metrics (guardrails), vanity vs. actionable.

## Phase Structure
- **Opening & Calibration (5 min):** "Walk me through your PM journey." Calibrating ownership scope, product types, team size.
- **Deep Behavioral (30-35 min):** 3-4 core questions, 8-10 min each. Product ownership, stakeholder conflict, data-driven decisions are non-negotiable topics.
- **Meta-Reflection (5-8 min):** "What's your product philosophy?" / "How has your PM approach changed?" Tests coherent PM worldview.
- **Candidate Questions (5 min):** Expect questions about strategy, team structure, decision culture — not logistics.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Ownership scope is the dividing line.** Own entire product surfaces end-to-end. Multiple shipped products with measurable outcomes. "I drove" not "I contributed to."
- **Stakeholder stories must involve real tension.** Genuine conflicting incentives (sales vs. engineering vs. user research), not generic "we disagreed then aligned."
- **Metrics fluency expected.** Naturally weave metrics into every story. "Increased 7-day retention by 12% while maintaining activation rate guardrail."
- **Framework articulation.** Name and explain prioritization framework (RICE, ICE, weighted scoring) with real application.
- **Amazon focus:** Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action are top 3 Leadership Principles tested.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Org design or team-of-teams structure
- Multi-year vision or market entry strategy (director+ scope)
- Hiring, coaching, developing other PMs
- P&L ownership or board-level communication
- Stories where candidate was purely project manager (tracking tasks, no product decisions)

## Probe Patterns
- Prioritization logic: "Exactly how did you decide what to build and cut? What framework?"
- Metrics depth: "North star metric? Counter-metric? How did you avoid gaming the primary?"
- Stakeholder motivation: "Why was that stakeholder pushing for that? Underlying concern?"
- Outcome validation: "Quantified impact? How did metric move after launch?"
- Scope isolation: "Who made that decision — you or your manager?"
