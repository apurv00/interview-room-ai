# Product Manager — Case Study Interview — Senior Level (7+ yr / GPM / Director / VP)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **New Market Entry** — "Should Google enter project management? Present to the CEO."
2. **Portfolio Strategy** — "4 product lines: one growing 80% YoY but unprofitable, one declining but profitable. Allocate resources."
3. **Platform vs. Point Solution** — "Team builds point solutions. CTO wants platform investment. Make the case."
4. **Build vs. Buy vs. Partner** — "Need ML capabilities. Evaluate build in-house, acquire startup, or partner with AWS."
5. **Turnaround** — "Product flatlined at 2M MAU for 18 months. CEO gives 1 year, $5M. Plan?"
6. **Multi-Product Synergy** — "Create synergies between Uber Rides, Eats, Freight without over-coupling."
7. **Monetization Strategy Shift** — "Board wants freemium → usage-based pricing. Walk through strategy."
8. **Org Design for Product** — "Inheriting 40-person product org. Structure teams around this portfolio."
9. **Disruption Response** — "AI startup commoditizing your core product. 3-year strategy?"
10. **Investment Memo / Product Vision** — "Write the 1-page product vision for next 3 years. Present it."

## Phase Structure
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Framing & Strategic Context | 3-5 min | Reframe in terms of company strategy, market dynamics, time horizon. Demonstrate you understand *why* this is being asked now. |
| Market Analysis | 4-5 min | TAM/SAM/SOM with reasoning. Competitive moats. Industry trends (regulatory, technology, behavioral). |
| Strategic Options | 5-7 min | 2-3 *strategies* (not features). Each with thesis, assumptions, resources, risk profile. |
| Recommendation with Conviction | 3-4 min | Choose one. Defend with first-principles. State what you're NOT doing and why. |
| Execution Roadmap | 4-5 min | Phase 0 (validate, 0-3mo), Phase 1 (MVP, 3-9mo), Phase 2 (scale, 9-18mo). Team + key hires. |
| Measurement & Governance | 3-4 min | Leading indicators per phase. Kill criteria — what data makes you abandon. Board reporting. |
| Second-Order Effects | 2-3 min | Impact on existing products, brand, talent, competitive response, regulatory. |
| Q&A / Stress Test | 5+ min | Interviewer plays skeptical board member or CEO. THIS is the test at senior levels. |

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Strategy, not features.** Never "design a feature." Make portfolio, market, or org decisions.
- **Ambiguity is the point.** Question deliberately under-specified. Ability to frame the problem IS the answer.
- **Conviction under uncertainty.** Mid-level PMs hedge. Senior PMs take a position, defend it, articulate conditions for changing mind.
- **Org and people awareness.** Solutions include team structure, hiring plans, cross-functional alignment. Can't ignore the human system.
- **Time horizons: 1-3 year arcs**, not sprints. Phased roadmaps with explicit gates and kill criteria.
- **CEO/board communication.** Structured like executive briefing (pyramid principle), not brainstorm.
- **Second-order thinking.** "If we do X, competitors respond with Y, so we pre-invest in Z."

## Common Problems
- Enter or exit a market (full strategic justification)
- Allocate resources across product portfolio
- Respond to disruptive competitor or technology
- Restructure product org around new strategy
- Major build/buy/partner with financial reasoning
- Present multi-year vision with phased execution

## Anti-Patterns
- Feature-level thinking ("build dashboard with 5 features" for a market entry question)
- Analysis paralysis (every option equally valid, no recommendation)
- Ignoring organizational reality (strategy requiring 3x headcount or culture change unaddressed)
- No kill criteria (no articulation of when to abandon)
- Underestimating competitive response (assuming static market)
- Abstract strategy without execution specifics ("invest in AI" without team/data/timeline)
- Avoiding hard tradeoffs (kill legacy product, lay off team, cannibalize revenue)

## Probe Patterns
- "CEO disagrees. Convince me in 60 seconds."
- "What makes you kill this 6 months in?"
- "Best PM just quit. How does plan change?"
- "Board wants ROI in 12, not 18 months. What changes?"
- "Competitor announces same strategy next week. Now what?"
- "How get VP Engineering — who owns legacy platform — to support?"
- "Biggest assumption? How de-risk it?"
- "Half the budget. What version still works?"
