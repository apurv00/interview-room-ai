# Product Manager — Case Study Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years / APM)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Product Critique** — "Pick a product you use daily. What would you improve?" (Google APM classic)
2. **Simple Feature Design** — "Design a feature for Instagram for college students"
3. **User-Centric Improvement** — "How would you improve Google Maps for tourists?"
4. **Metric Diagnosis** — "DAU dropped 10% on a feature. Walk me through why."
5. **Basic Prioritization** — "3 feature ideas, 1 engineer. How do you decide?"
6. **Product Teardown** — "What makes TikTok's onboarding work? What doesn't?"
7. **Simple Go-to-Market** — "Launching a notes app for students. How?"
8. **Estimation-Adjacent** — "How many searches does Google Maps handle daily?"

## Phase Structure
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Clarify | 2-3 min | Ask about users, constraints, platform. Show you don't jump in. |
| User Segmentation | 3-4 min | Identify 2-3 segments, pick one with reasoning. |
| Pain Points | 3-4 min | List 3-5 pain points. Prioritize by severity and frequency. |
| Solution Brainstorm | 4-5 min | Generate 3 solutions. Briefly describe each. |
| Prioritize & Recommend | 3-4 min | Pick one using simple framework (impact vs. effort). Justify. |
| Metrics | 2-3 min | 1 north-star metric + 2-3 supporting metrics. |
| Wrap-Up | 1-2 min | Summarize, acknowledge tradeoffs. |

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Tests **structured thinking**, not domain expertise. Want a repeatable framework.
- Heavy emphasis on **user empathy** — name real people and real pain points, not abstract personas.
- "Improve X" prompts dominate (70%+ of APM case studies per Exponent data).
- Interviewers want to see you **self-scope** by choosing a segment and committing.
- **Creativity matters more than business sophistication.** Novel user insight beats perfect TAM calculation.

## Common Problems
- Improve an existing consumer product (Maps, Spotify, YouTube)
- Design a simple feature for a defined audience
- Diagnose a single-metric drop with structured tree
- Basic product critique with actionable recommendations

## Anti-Patterns
- Framework robot: Reciting "users, pain points, solutions, metrics" mechanically without insight
- Boiling the ocean: Solving for all users instead of picking one segment
- Solution-first: "Add AI!" before understanding the problem
- Ignoring tradeoffs: One solution as perfect, no downsides
- Metric hand-waving: "Track engagement" without defining concretely
- No personal opinion: So structured you never express product conviction

## Probe Patterns
- "Why that user segment over the others?"
- "Biggest risk with your solution?"
- "How know if working after 1 month?"
- "What cut if engineering says 3x longer?"
- "A PM disagrees. How handle?"
