# Product Manager — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ yr / GPM / Director / VP)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Product Vision & Multi-Year Strategy** — "Tell me about a product vision you championed over multiple planning cycles." Strategies spanning 1-3 years. Market positioning, competitive moats, long-term user value.
2. **Building & Scaling a Product Organization** — "How you built or scaled a product team." Team boundaries, management layers, specialists vs. generalists. VP scope includes design, research, operations.
3. **Executive Stakeholder Alignment** — "Aligning executive team on a controversial product direction." VP vs. SVP/CEO dynamics. Hold ground with data while maintaining executive trust.
4. **Turning Around Underperforming Product or Team** — "Product that was failing and how you changed its trajectory." Diagnose root cause (wrong strategy, people, process, or market?), make hard decisions.
5. **Cross-Functional Leadership at Scale** — "Aligning product, engineering, sales, marketing on major initiative." 50-200+ person efforts. Led through systems (OKRs, planning rituals) not just relationships.
6. **People Leadership & PM Development** — "How you've developed PMs." Coaching frameworks, career conversations, performance management. VP: building PM culture that scales.
7. **Market Strategy & Business Impact** — "Identified new market opportunity → product strategy." P&L dynamics, unit economics, market sizing, competitive positioning. Material revenue/market-share impact.
8. **Making Hard Tradeoffs with Incomplete Information** — "Hardest product decision ever." Existential tradeoffs: which market, which product to sunset, whether to pivot.
9. **Navigating Organizational Politics** — "Politically sensitive product decision." Navigate transparently with data and user advocacy, not backroom maneuvering.
10. **Legacy & Impact Reflection** — "Biggest product achievement and why?" / "Decision you'd make differently." Durable impact vs. launch celebrations.

## Phase Structure
- **Strategic Framing (10 min):** "Walk me through your product leadership journey and biggest strategic bets." Not standard "tell me about yourself" — strategic narrative assessment showing increasing scope.
- **Leadership Deep Dive (25-30 min):** 2-3 deep questions, 10-12 min each. Organizational leadership, executive alignment, strategic decision-making.
- **Business Acumen Test (10 min):** Market strategy, P&L impact, or build/buy/partner. May become a mini case study at VP level.
- **Meta & Culture (5-8 min):** "How do you build product culture?" Tests developed philosophy for executive audience.
- **Candidate Questions (5 min):** Actively evaluated. Expect strategy, market position, organizational challenges.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Stories must operate at org/company scope.** Individual feature stories are disqualifying. Multi-team coordination, multi-quarter timelines, company-level impact.
- **Impact extends beyond product function.** GPM: +design, engineering. Director: +sales, marketing. VP: +support, operations, partnerships.
- **People leadership is non-negotiable.** "I built the team" matters as much as "I built the product." Team design, hiring calibration, hard people decisions.
- **Business and financial fluency.** Revenue, margins, market share, TAM, unit economics, competitive dynamics. User empathy alone insufficient.
- **Enduring strategy over execution stories.** "What strategy did you set and how did it compound over time."
- **Organizational design is first-class.** Team structure, roles, planning cadences, decision frameworks as important as products built.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Individual feature prioritization or sprint decisions (IC PM concerns)
- "Worked with an engineer to solve a bug" (wrong scope)
- Personal time management or multiple deadlines (mid-level territory)
- Product sense exercises ("design a product for X") — IC interview component
- Daily standups, agile ceremonies, process mechanics
- Stories where candidate executed someone else's strategy

## Probe Patterns
- Strategy durability: "How did that hold up over 12-18 months? What changed in the market?"
- Org design rationale: "Why that team structure? When add management layer? What differently?"
- Executive influence: "How did you get CEO/board on board? Data in that conversation? When exec disagreed?"
- People decisions: "Had to let a PM go or move off project? Strong IC who didn't want management?"
- Scope of impact: "Revenue/market-share impact? How did this change competitive position?"
- Counter-factual: "What if you'd made the other choice? Argument against your strategy?"
- Culture building: "How would your team describe your leadership? Rituals that outlasted you?"
