# Design / UX — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ years / Design Lead / Director)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Design Vision & Org Building** — "How have you established or reshaped the design culture at a company?" Tests ability to articulate and operationalize a design philosophy — not just do good design, but build an environment where good design happens systematically.
2. **Executive Influence & Strategic Alignment** — "Tell me about influencing a C-suite executive or VP on a design-driven initiative." At this level, design must have a seat at the strategy table — tests ability to speak the language of business outcomes, not just user empathy.
3. **Design System Strategy** — "How have you approached design system adoption and governance across multiple product teams?" Tests systems thinking, balancing consistency with team autonomy, and change management at scale.
4. **Building & Growing a Design Team** — "Tell me about building a design team — hiring, structuring, and developing designers." Tests leadership maturity, hiring philosophy, mentorship approach, and ability to create career growth paths.
5. **Cross-Functional Leadership at Scale** — "Tell me about aligning design, product, and engineering leadership on a strategic bet." Tests ability to navigate organizational politics, resolve competing priorities, and build coalition among peer leaders.
6. **Design Metrics & Business Impact** — "How do you measure design's contribution to business outcomes?" Tests ability to define and track design KPIs (NPS, SUS, task success, conversion, retention) and connect them to revenue, growth, or strategic goals.
7. **Managing Through Ambiguity & Change** — "Tell me about leading your team through a major pivot, reorg, or strategic shift." Tests resilience, communication, maintaining team morale and quality during uncertainty.
8. **Design Culture & Quality Standards** — "How do you ensure design quality scales as the team and product grow?" Tests approach to critique culture, design reviews, quality gates, and balancing speed with craft.

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-Up (3-5 min):** Brief arc — career trajectory, current scope (team size, products, report chain). How do they frame their impact at org level?
- **Leadership & Strategy (15-20 min):** 2-3 deep questions on vision, executive influence, org building. 8-10 min each. Expect cross-company examples and systems-level thinking.
- **Team & Culture (10-15 min):** 1-2 questions on hiring, mentorship, critique culture, design operations. How they talk about their team reveals leadership character.
- **Impact & Measurement (5-8 min):** How they quantify design's value to the business. Expect frameworks (HEART, SUS, NPS, design-specific OKRs).
- **Candidate Questions (5 min):** Senior candidates ask about the design org's reporting structure, executive sponsorship, product strategy, and what success looks like for this role in 12 months.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Soft skills outweigh hard skills.** At design director level, how you lead, influence, and build culture matters more than your ability to push pixels. The interview is more like a leadership conversation than a skills assessment.
- **Business fluency is non-negotiable.** Must connect design to revenue, retention, brand equity, and competitive positioning. "It looked better" is not impact.
- **They evaluate how you talk about your team.** Leaders who say "I" when describing team work are red flags. Leaders who say "my team achieved X and here's how I enabled it" signal strong leadership character.
- **Organizational design is part of the job.** Hub-and-spoke vs. embedded model, designer-to-PM ratios, centralized vs. decentralized research — expect questions about team topology.
- **Design ops and process maturity expected.** Scalable critique processes, design-to-dev handoff workflows, component library governance, design token systems.
- **Vision must be concrete, not aspirational.** "I believe in user-centered design" is insufficient. "I implemented monthly design reviews with product leadership using a quality rubric tied to our NPS targets" is the bar.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Tool-specific questions (Figma features, prototyping mechanics)
- Individual contributor design execution (wireframing a specific flow)
- Basic design principles or UCD process steps
- Portfolio walkthrough of individual project details
- Accessibility implementation specifics (vs. accessibility strategy)
- Design challenge or whiteboard exercise (inappropriate for director-level)

## Probe Patterns
- Vision concreteness: "You mentioned building design culture — what specific rituals or processes did you introduce? How did you measure their effectiveness?"
- Executive influence: "The VP disagreed — walk me through the conversation. What data changed their mind?"
- Team building: "How do you decide between hiring a senior IC versus promoting from within?"
- Difficult people leadership: "Tell me about a designer on your team who wasn't performing. How did you handle it?"
- Metrics rigor: "You track NPS — how do you isolate design's contribution from product and engineering changes?"
- Scaling quality: "Your team doubled in size. How did design quality change? What did you do about it?"
- Strategic tradeoffs: "When the CEO says 'ship faster,' how do you protect design quality?"
- Org design: "Why did you choose embedded designers over a centralized studio model? What were the tradeoffs?"
