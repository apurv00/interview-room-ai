# Design / UX — Technical Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Design Fundamentals** — "Explain the difference between UX and UI. How do they work together?" Tests foundational vocabulary and conceptual clarity. Expect follow-ups on hierarchy, Gestalt principles, affordances, and mental models.
2. **Design Process Walkthrough** — "Walk me through your design process for a recent project, from research to delivery." Tests knowledge of the UCD cycle: research, personas, ideation, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, iteration. Process matters more than output quality.
3. **Portfolio Deep Dive** — "Show me this project. Walk me through your design decisions." Interviewer probes specific choices: Why this layout? Why this interaction pattern? What alternatives did you consider? Tests articulation of rationale, not just aesthetics.
4. **Tool Proficiency (Figma/Sketch)** — "How do you organize your Figma files? Explain components vs. variants." Tests practical working knowledge: auto layout, component libraries, design tokens, naming conventions, handoff preparation. Not mastery — foundational competence.
5. **Basic Prototyping & Interaction Design** — "How would you prototype this flow? What interactions would you add?" Tests understanding of micro-interactions, transitions, states (hover, active, disabled, error), and how to communicate behavior to developers.
6. **Accessibility Basics** — "What accessibility considerations would you apply to this design?" Tests awareness of WCAG basics: color contrast (4.5:1), keyboard navigation, alt text, focus indicators, touch targets (44px min). Not expert — but aware and intentional.
7. **User Research Fundamentals** — "What research methods would you use to validate this design?" Tests knowledge of usability testing, user interviews, surveys, card sorting, tree testing. Can they match method to question?
8. **Design Critique** — "Here's an app/screen — what works and what would you improve?" Tests critical eye, ability to evaluate design against heuristics (Nielsen's 10), and constructive articulation of improvements.

## Phase Structure
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Portfolio Walkthrough | 15-20 min | 1-2 projects in depth. Interviewer probes decisions, alternatives considered, constraints, and learnings. |
| Design Fundamentals | 10-15 min | Conceptual questions on UX principles, interaction patterns, accessibility, information architecture. |
| Tool & Process Check | 5-8 min | Figma/Sketch organization, prototyping approach, handoff practices. May be a quick practical exercise. |
| App Critique (optional) | 5-10 min | Given a real app to evaluate. Tests critical thinking and design vocabulary. |

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Evaluated on thinking, not mastery.** Interviewers assess clarity of reasoning, design vocabulary, and structured approach — not years of shipped products. A thoughtful junior beats a sloppy mid-level.
- **Portfolio IS the technical exam.** For entry-level, the portfolio walkthrough serves as the primary technical assessment. Case studies must document the full process (research, wireframes, iterations, final designs), not just polished mockups.
- **Fundamentals over frameworks.** Know hierarchy, contrast, alignment, proximity, consistency. Know when to use a modal vs. a toast vs. inline validation. Don't need to name every heuristic — but apply them intuitively.
- **Figma competence is expected.** Components, variants, auto layout, basic prototyping. Not design systems architecture — but clean file organization and reusable components signal professionalism.
- **Accessibility awareness, not expertise.** Know that color contrast matters, touch targets need minimum sizes, screen readers exist. Not expected to audit WCAG 2.2 AA compliance in detail.
- **Research methods knowledge, not practice depth.** Can name and describe usability testing, card sorting, A/B testing, user interviews. May not have extensive practice — but understands when each applies.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Design system architecture, token systems, or multi-platform governance
- Advanced research methodology (diary studies, contextual inquiry, statistical analysis)
- Design operations, tooling decisions, or process optimization at scale
- Accessibility auditing or WCAG 2.2 AAA compliance deep dives
- Advanced prototyping (code-based prototypes, Framer, complex animations)
- Design strategy, product vision, or competitive analysis
- Cross-team design consistency or brand system decisions
- Expecting production-quality visual design in portfolio

## Probe Patterns
- Design rationale: "Why did you choose a bottom sheet instead of a full-screen modal here?"
- Alternative thinking: "What other approaches did you consider? Why did you reject them?"
- Constraint awareness: "What were the technical or time constraints? How did they shape the design?"
- Accessibility check: "What happens to this design for a color-blind user? A screen reader user?"
- Iteration evidence: "Show me an earlier version. What changed and why?"
- Research connection: "How did your user research findings influence this specific design decision?"
- Critique constructiveness: "You said this app's navigation is confusing — how would you fix it? Sketch it out."
