# Design / UX — Technical Interview — Senior Level (7+ years / Design Lead / Director)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Design Strategy & Vision** — "How do you develop and communicate a design strategy for a product line or business unit?" Tests ability to connect design work to business objectives, define a multi-quarter design roadmap, and align it with product and engineering strategy. Not just "make things user-friendly" — a concrete, measurable plan.
2. **Design Operations & Process at Scale** — "How have you established design processes that scale across multiple teams?" Tests design ops maturity: critique cadence, review gates, handoff standards, design-to-dev cycle time, file/component governance, onboarding new designers. Hub-and-spoke governance models, RFC processes, monthly governance syncs.
3. **Design System Governance & Architecture** — "You have 8 product teams using a shared design system. How do you govern contributions, handle breaking changes, and measure adoption?" Tests systems thinking at org scale: contribution workflows, versioning, deprecation policies, adoption metrics (component usage, override rates), and balancing team autonomy with consistency.
4. **Measuring Design Impact (Quantitative Frameworks)** — "How do you prove design's value to the business? What metrics do you own?" Tests ability to define and defend design KPIs: HEART framework (Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task success), SUS scores, NPS, task completion rates, error rates, and connecting these to business metrics (conversion, revenue, LTV, churn).
5. **Scaling Design Quality** — "Your team tripled in size. Design quality is inconsistent. What's your approach?" Tests quality systems: design reviews with rubrics, pair design, critique culture, pattern libraries, documentation standards, and quality metrics that catch regression before shipping.
6. **Research Strategy & Insights Infrastructure** — "How do you build a research practice that informs product decisions at scale?" Tests ability to establish research operations: research repository (Dovetail, EnjoyHQ), insight taxonomies, research democratization vs. specialization, longitudinal studies, and connecting research to product roadmap decisions.
7. **Accessibility Strategy** — "How do you move an organization from 'accessibility as compliance' to 'accessibility as competitive advantage'?" Tests strategic approach to inclusive design: audit programs, training, hiring, VPAT/compliance, executive buy-in, embedding accessibility into design system tokens and components, and measuring progress.
8. **Design Technology & Innovation** — "How do you evaluate and adopt new design tools, AI-assisted design, or emerging interaction paradigms for your team?" Tests forward-thinking leadership: evaluating AI prototyping tools, design-to-code workflows, spatial computing, voice interfaces — and making pragmatic adoption decisions vs. chasing trends.

## Phase Structure
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Design Strategy & Vision | 10-15 min | How they develop, communicate, and execute design strategy. Expect multi-quarter thinking tied to business OKRs. |
| Design Ops & Systems | 10-15 min | Governance models, process design, tooling decisions, team topology. Expect specific examples of what they built and why. |
| Design Metrics & Impact | 8-10 min | Quantitative frameworks for measuring design's business impact. HEART, SUS, NPS, and custom design OKRs. |
| Research & Accessibility Strategy | 8-10 min | Research operations, insights infrastructure, accessibility program design. |
| Design Innovation | 5 min | Forward-looking perspective on design tools, AI, emerging paradigms. |

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Strategy over execution.** Interviewers evaluate systems thinking, organizational design, and business impact measurement — not individual design craft. A senior candidate who talks mostly about their own designs is signaling IC, not leadership.
- **90-day plan articulation.** Strong candidates can describe their approach to a new leadership role: Listen (stakeholder interviews, design ops audit), Align (vision synthesis tied to business OKRs), Activate (pilots, working groups, 3-year roadmap with governance).
- **Design ops is a core competency.** Not an afterthought — design specification completeness, review-cycle duration targets (<48 hours), component adoption rates, and pixel-parity defect rates post-launch.
- **Metrics fluency is mandatory.** Must define multi-tiered measurement: business tier (revenue, LTV, churn), experience tier (SUS, task success, NPS), operational tier (cycle time, component reuse). Must connect design metrics to metrics the board already tracks.
- **Hub-and-spoke governance expected.** Central Design Operations hub owns the system and tooling, with an RFC process for decisions, and regular governance syncs reviewing adoption and violations.
- **Accessibility is strategic, not tactical.** Expected to champion organization-wide accessibility programs, not just check WCAG boxes on individual projects.
- **AI and emerging tech perspective.** Expected to have a thoughtful position on AI-assisted design, design-to-code tools, and new interaction paradigms — neither dismissive nor hype-driven.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Individual design execution tasks (wireframe this flow, prototype that interaction)
- Tool-level questions (Figma features, prototyping mechanics)
- Basic design principles, UCD process steps, or design vocabulary
- Portfolio walkthrough of individual projects in detail
- App critique exercises (appropriate for mid-level, not director-level)
- Accessibility implementation specifics (vs. accessibility program strategy)
- Design challenge or whiteboard exercise

## Probe Patterns
- Strategy concreteness: "You mentioned a design strategy — show me the artifact. How did you get executive buy-in?"
- Ops rigor: "What's your target for design-to-dev cycle time? How do you measure it? What happened when teams missed it?"
- System governance: "A product team wants to fork the design system for a new market. How do you handle that request?"
- Metrics defense: "The CEO asks 'what's the ROI of the design team?' Walk me through your answer with specific numbers."
- Quality scaling: "You have 5 designers shipping features in parallel. How do you catch quality regressions before they ship?"
- Research strategy: "How do you decide between investing in foundational research vs. tactical usability testing?"
- Accessibility program: "What's your approach to getting engineering teams to prioritize accessibility fixes when they have a feature backlog?"
- Team topology: "You're starting fresh — embedded designers per squad or centralized studio? Walk me through your reasoning."
