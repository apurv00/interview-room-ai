# Business & Strategy — Technical Interview — Senior Level (7+ years / Principal / Director / VP Strategy)

NOTE: Senior business/strategy "technical" tests ability to design analytical infrastructure, evaluate strategic investments, and make enterprise-level financial decisions — not execute individual analyses.

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Investment Thesis & Strategic Due Diligence** — "We're evaluating a $500M acquisition. Walk me through your due diligence framework." Tests ability to structure comprehensive assessment: Quality of Earnings, Net Working Capital, Indebtedness, market position, integration risks, and synergy realization. Must distinguish between deal-breakers and manageable risks.
2. **P&L Ownership & Financial Strategy** — "You own a $200M business unit. Revenue is flat, margins are compressing. Walk me through your 3-year turnaround plan." Tests CEO-level financial thinking: revenue diversification, cost restructuring, investment prioritization, and Board communication of the plan.
3. **Portfolio Strategy & Capital Allocation** — "The company has 5 business units. Which do you invest in, which do you divest, and why?" Tests McKinsey Three Horizons thinking, BCG growth-share matrix logic, and ability to make portfolio decisions that balance short-term performance with long-term positioning.
4. **Strategic Planning Framework Design** — "Design the annual strategic planning process for a $2B company." Tests ability to build institutional systems — not just do strategy, but create the process that produces strategy. Governance, cadence, stakeholder engagement, metrics cascade.
5. **M&A Integration & Value Capture** — "Day 1 after acquisition close. Walk me through your 100-day integration plan." Tests practical M&A experience: cultural integration, operational synergy capture, customer retention, talent retention, and communication protocols. PE-style "actionable 100-day plan with measurable EBITDA growth."
6. **Organizational Design for Strategic Execution** — "The strategy requires moving from product-centric to customer-centric operating model. Design the org transition." Tests understanding that strategy fails more often from organizational misalignment than from wrong strategy. Spans and layers, governance, incentive structures.
7. **Advanced Competitive Strategy & Market Dynamics** — "A well-funded competitor is disrupting your core market. Design the competitive response." Tests game-theoretic thinking: anticipating competitor moves, defensive vs. offensive posture, build/buy/partner decisions, and timing of response.
8. **Board & Investor Communication** — "Prepare a board memo on a strategic pivot that will reduce near-term revenue by 20% but position the company for long-term growth." Tests ability to frame strategic tradeoffs for a governance audience — concise, data-backed, with explicit risk/reward and resource requests.

## Phase Structure
- **Phase 1 (0-10 min):** Complex scenario framing — multi-business-unit company facing strategic crossroads. Candidate must ask clarifying questions to scope the problem.
- **Phase 2 (10-25 min):** Core analytical / strategic exercise — portfolio analysis, M&A evaluation, or turnaround plan. Interviewer probes continuously on assumptions, alternatives, and implications.
- **Phase 3 (25-40 min):** Organizational and implementation dimension — how to execute, what to communicate to whom, what can go wrong. This is where senior separates from mid.
- **Phase 4 (40-50 min):** Board/executive communication — "Pitch this to the board in 3 minutes." Tests distillation of complex analysis into executive-ready narrative.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Questions shift from "analyze this" to "design the system for ongoing analysis and decision-making."** The answer is a process, governance framework, or organizational capability — not a one-time analysis.
- **Financial sophistication is assumed, not tested in isolation.** Nobody asks "walk me through a DCF" at this level. Instead, financial reasoning is embedded in strategic questions — "What discount rate would make this acquisition value-destructive?"
- **Experience-formed opinions are expected.** "I don't know but I'd research it" is no longer sufficient. Must have a point of view — "In my experience, revenue synergies realize at 30-50% of projections in the first 2 years, so I'd haircut aggressively."
- **Organizational dynamics are inseparable from strategy.** Every strategic recommendation must address: who will resist it, why, and how you'll manage that resistance.
- **Three types of due diligence:** Financial (Quality of Earnings, NWC, Indebtedness), Commercial (market position, customer concentration, competitive dynamics), and Operational (integration complexity, technology debt, talent risk).
- **PE/VC interview format at this level:** Candidates present actionable 100-day plans with measurable EBITDA growth potential. Must demonstrate rapid operational turnaround, digital transformation, and M&A integration experience.

## Anti-Patterns
- Same analytical depth as mid-level — executing analysis instead of designing analytical frameworks
- Strategy without implementation plan — the slide deck is not the answer
- Ignoring organizational resistance and change management
- All investments framed as "obviously worth it" or "obviously not" — must articulate evaluation framework
- Financial analysis without strategic context (analyst-level DCF without CEO-level "so what")
- Recommendations that don't scale — thinking single-unit when the question is multi-BU
- Ignoring board/investor dynamics and communication requirements

## Probe Patterns
- "The board wants a single number for 'deal health.' What do you give them? What do you refuse to collapse into one metric?"
- "Integration is 9 months behind plan. Revenue synergies aren't materializing. What do you do?"
- "The CEO of the acquired company disagrees with your integration approach. How does that conversation go?"
- "This investment won't show impact for 18 months. How do you justify it to a quarterly-focused board?"
- "Two business units have conflicting strategic priorities that can't both be funded. How do you resolve?"
- "The chief strategy officer left 3 months ago. The planning process has stalled. Walk me through your first 30 days."
