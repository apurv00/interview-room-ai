# Business & Strategy — Case Study Interview — Senior Level (7+ years / Principal / Partner / VP Strategy / Director)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **CEO-Level Strategic Transformation** — "The board has asked you to design a 3-year transformation to reposition a $5B industrial company for a digital-first future. Present your strategy." THE signature senior case. Tests ability to operate at CEO agenda level: vision setting, portfolio reconfiguration, organizational redesign, investment sequencing, stakeholder management, and board communication. Not a case to solve — a strategy to design.
2. **Portfolio Rationalization & Capital Allocation** — "A conglomerate with 6 divisions generates 80% of profit from 2 divisions. The board wants a portfolio strategy. What do you recommend?" Tests portfolio-level thinking: divest underperformers vs. restructure, capital reallocation logic, Three Horizons framework, strategic fit assessment, and tax/regulatory implications of divestiture.
3. **Large-Scale M&A & Integration** — "Your client wants to acquire their largest competitor for $2B. Walk through the strategic logic, deal structure, and first-year integration plan." Tests end-to-end M&A capability: strategic rationale, valuation (with synergy modeling), financing structure, anti-trust considerations, Day 1 planning, 100-day integration roadmap, and value capture tracking.
4. **Market Disruption Response** — "A well-funded tech entrant with $500M in VC funding is commoditizing your client's core product. The CEO has 18 months of runway before material revenue impact. Design the response." Tests strategic under time pressure: defend, disrupt yourself, acquire the disruptor, or pivot. Must address speed vs. thoroughness, organizational mobilization, and board alignment.
5. **Organizational Restructuring** — "Post-merger, the combined entity has redundant functions, conflicting cultures, and declining employee engagement. Design the restructuring." Tests organizational design expertise: spans and layers optimization, governance redesign, cultural integration, talent retention strategy, and communication planning. Strategy fails from org misalignment more than wrong analysis.
6. **Growth Through Innovation Portfolio** — "A mature CPG company needs to build its innovation pipeline. Design the innovation strategy." Tests Three Horizons application: Horizon 1 (core optimization), Horizon 2 (adjacencies), Horizon 3 (new ventures). Must address funding model, governance, metrics (different for each horizon), and organizational structure for innovation.
7. **Geopolitical / Regulatory Strategy** — "New trade regulations will increase input costs by 30% for your client's China-dependent supply chain. Design the strategic response." Tests ability to handle macro-level constraints: supply chain diversification, near-shoring economics, regulatory lobbying, scenario planning under geopolitical uncertainty, and board risk communication.
8. **Private Equity Value Creation** — "You're the operating partner for a PE portfolio company. EBITDA is flat, the fund needs 3x in 4 years. Design the value creation plan." Tests PE-style thinking: revenue acceleration, margin expansion, add-on acquisitions, working capital optimization, management team upgrade, and exit preparation. Must present actionable 100-day plan.

## Phase Structure
- **Phase 1 — Strategic Context (0-10 min):** Complex, multi-dimensional scenario presented — often a real (anonymized) engagement the interviewing partner worked on. Candidate must ask incisive scoping questions that demonstrate strategic sophistication. Quality of questions matters as much as answers.
- **Phase 2 — Framework & Hypothesis (10-20 min):** No off-the-shelf frameworks. Candidate designs a bespoke analytical approach that integrates financial, organizational, and market dimensions. Must articulate upfront hypotheses and explain sequencing logic.
- **Phase 3 — Strategic Analysis & Debate (20-35 min):** Conversational analysis with the interviewing partner. Data may or may not be provided — candidate must state what data they'd need and what they'd do with partial information. Interviewer challenges assumptions, introduces curveballs, and tests adaptability.
- **Phase 4 — Board Pitch (35-45 min):** "You have 5 minutes with the board. Pitch your recommendation." Tests distillation of complex multi-dimensional analysis into executive-ready narrative: clear recommendation, quantified impact, key risks, resource requirements, and timeline.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **The case IS the conversation.** No structured 4-step framework exercise. Partners use cases from real engagements and discuss them as peers. Rigid structure signals "not ready for partner track."
- **Interviewers are partners — they ask whatever they find interesting.** Less prescriptive than lower levels. May challenge your industry thesis, ask about a tangential market, or test intellectual range on unrelated strategic questions.
- **Cases are often drawn from the interviewer's own experience.** They know the answer intimately. The test is whether you'd reach similar conclusions (or better ones) through your own reasoning.
- **Organizational and implementation dimensions are mandatory.** A strategy without a restructuring plan, change management approach, or stakeholder alignment strategy is incomplete. "The answer" is 30% analytics and 70% execution design.
- **Board-ready communication is tested explicitly.** Must frame complex tradeoffs concisely: "We're recommending a $200M investment that reduces near-term margins by 300bp but positions us to capture $1.5B in market share over 5 years. Key risk is execution speed — here's our mitigation."
- **Point of view and conviction are expected.** "I don't know" is unacceptable at partner level. Must have experience-formed perspectives: "In my experience, revenue synergies realize at 30-50% of projections in year one, so I'd haircut aggressively."
- **No time for throat-clearing.** The best senior candidates go directly to the strategic insight in the first 2 minutes, then structure around it. Starting with "Let me draw a 2x2 matrix" signals junior thinking.

## Common Problems
- Full corporate transformation strategy (industrial → digital)
- Conglomerate portfolio rationalization (invest/divest/restructure)
- Large-cap M&A evaluation and integration design
- Competitive response to venture-backed disruptor
- Post-merger organizational restructuring
- Innovation portfolio design (Three Horizons)
- Geopolitical supply chain restructuring
- PE value creation plan with measurable EBITDA targets

## Anti-Patterns
- Treating the case like a junior exercise (structured 4-step framework walk-through)
- Strategy without implementation — the slide deck is not the deliverable
- Ignoring organizational resistance, cultural integration, and change management
- Over-reliance on financial analysis without strategic narrative
- Recommendations that work for one business unit but not the enterprise
- No point of view — waiting for data instead of forming hypotheses
- Generic frameworks (Porter's Five Forces as main analytical tool for a restructuring)
- Missing the "so what for the board" — analysis without executive-ready framing
- Solving the presenting symptom instead of the structural cause

## Probe Patterns
- "The CEO loves this strategy. The CFO thinks it's too risky. The board is split. How do you navigate?"
- "You're 6 months into execution and results are 40% below projections. What do you do?"
- "The acquisition target's CEO demands to stay. She's brilliant but culturally misaligned. Your recommendation?"
- "This strategy requires $200M over 3 years. The company has $300M of available capital and 4 competing priorities. How do you frame the choice?"
- "Your recommendation requires firing 2,000 people. How do you present that to the board? To the employees? To the press?"
- "A board member asks: 'What's the probability this works?' How do you answer?"
- "You disagree with the CEO's preferred strategy. They've been in the industry 30 years. How do you handle it?"
- "If you're wrong about this recommendation, what's the cost? What's your hedge?"
