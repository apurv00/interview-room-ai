# Business & Strategy — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ years / Principal / Partner / VP Strategy / Director)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Building & Leading a Practice or Function** — "Tell me about building something from scratch — a practice, team, capability, or market offering." THE signature senior-level question. Tests entrepreneurial leadership at organizational scale. Not running a project — creating an institution.
2. **Executive Stakeholder & Board Communication** — "Describe how you communicate strategic decisions to a board or C-suite audience." Tests tailored communication — dashboards, concise risk framing, explicit resource requests. Not just presenting — shaping decisions.
3. **P&L Ownership & Revenue Impact** — "Tell me about a time you were accountable for a revenue target or P&L. What happened when it was at risk?" Tests commercial acumen and comfort with financial accountability. Partner-track candidates must prove they can sell and deliver.
4. **Organizational Transformation & Change Leadership** — "Describe leading an organization through a major change — restructuring, transformation, or strategic pivot." Tests ability to lead through resistance at scale, not just manage a project. Must address culture, communication, and sustained execution.
5. **Shaping Culture & Norms** — "How do you build the culture of a team or organization?" Tests whether candidate shapes norms through behavior, hiring, rituals, and decision-making — not just states values.
6. **Strategic Partnership & Business Development** — "Tell me about building a strategic relationship that created significant value for the firm/organization." VP/Partner level = relationship-driven revenue. Tests long-term relationship building, not transactional selling.
7. **Making Hard Calls Under Uncertainty** — "Tell me about a high-stakes decision you made with incomplete information where the consequences were significant." Tests decision-making framework, risk tolerance calibration, and comfort with irreversibility.
8. **Developing the Next Generation of Leaders** — "How have you identified, developed, and promoted talent in your organization?" Tests whether candidate builds leadership pipelines, not just manages direct reports.
9. **Navigating Failure at Scale** — "Tell me about a significant professional failure and how you handled the aftermath." At this level, failures have organizational consequences. Tests accountability, recovery, and institutional learning.

## Phase Structure
- **Extended Career Narrative (10-15 min):** Deep dive into career arc with emphasis on leadership trajectory, business impact, and strategic choices. Interviewer (often a partner or VP) is assessing judgment and maturity, not just accomplishments.
- **Strategic Leadership Scenarios (20-25 min):** 2-3 deep explorations of practice building, client portfolio management, or organizational transformation. Less structured than mid-level — more conversational, testing peer-level judgment.
- **Vision & Strategic Fit (10 min):** "Where do you want to take this practice/function in 3-5 years?" "What's your thesis on where the market is going?" Tests strategic vision and firm/org alignment.
- **Reverse Interview (10 min):** At this level, the candidate's questions are as diagnostic as the interviewer's — testing strategic thinking about the role, firm dynamics, and market opportunity.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Questions shift from "solving problems" to "building systems and organizations."** Answer is about creating frameworks, practices, or cultures — not heroic individual contribution.
- **Interviewer is assessing peer fit.** Partners and VPs are evaluating: "Would I trust this person to manage a $10M+ client relationship? To represent us to a board?"
- **Commercial track record is non-negotiable.** Must demonstrate revenue generation, business development wins, or P&L ownership with real numbers.
- **Thought leadership and market point of view expected.** "What do you believe about where this industry is heading that most people get wrong?" Having a thesis matters.
- **Stories must feature organizational-scale impact.** Team of 50+, budget of millions, multi-year timeline. Individual contributor stories disqualify.
- **Board and C-suite relationship sophistication required.** Not just presenting to executives — influencing their decisions, managing their expectations, delivering bad news constructively.
- **Final round is often informal dinner or extended conversation.** Culture fit and "partner material" assessment happens in unstructured settings.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Individual contributor tasks without organizational scope
- Basic teamwork or collaboration without leadership dimension
- Project execution without strategic context or business impact
- Learning from feedback (entry-level topic)
- Technical skill demonstration (appropriate for technical round, not behavioral)
- Client management at workstream level (mid-level topic)

## Probe Patterns
- Scale validation: "How large was the team/budget/revenue impact?"
- Strategic judgment: "What was your decision-making framework? How did you weigh the risks?"
- Board communication: "How did you frame this for the board? What questions did they push back on?"
- Culture building: "How did you make this stick? What happened after you left the room?"
- Revenue generation: "Walk me through how you won that client. What was your approach to the relationship?"
- Failure accountability: "What did the organization change as a result of that failure? What did YOU change?"
- Succession planning: "Who did you develop who is now in a leadership role? What was your approach?"
