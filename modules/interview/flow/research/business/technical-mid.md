# Business & Strategy — Technical Interview — Mid Level (3-6 years / Senior Associate / Engagement Manager)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Financial Modeling & Scenario Analysis** — "Build a 3-year projection for this SaaS company. What assumptions drive the model? Run an upside and downside scenario." Tests ability to build and stress-test models, not just fill in templates. Must articulate which assumptions matter most and why.
2. **Valuation Fundamentals** — "Walk me through how you'd value a mid-market consumer goods company." Tests DCF, comparable company multiples (EV/EBITDA, P/E), and precedent transactions. Must know when each method is appropriate and its limitations.
3. **Competitive Analysis & Strategic Positioning** — "Analyze the competitive landscape for [industry]. Where are the opportunities?" Tests application of Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, and competitive benchmarking with real data. Must produce actionable insight, not just framework recitation.
4. **Advanced Data Analysis & Insight Generation** — "Here's 6 months of operational data. Identify the 3 most impactful findings and recommend actions." Tests ability to go beyond descriptive analytics to diagnostic and prescriptive insight. Must prioritize findings by business impact.
5. **Market Entry / Growth Strategy Quantification** — "Should this company enter the Southeast Asian market? Size the opportunity and assess the investment." Tests integration of market sizing with financial analysis — total addressable market, competitive dynamics, required investment, and expected ROI.
6. **Pricing Strategy & Revenue Optimization** — "This B2B SaaS company is losing deals on price. Design a pricing analysis." Tests understanding of price elasticity, value-based pricing, competitive positioning, and willingness-to-pay research methods.
7. **Operational Metrics & KPI Design** — "You're advising a logistics company on performance improvement. Design the KPI dashboard." Tests ability to define leading vs. lagging indicators, set targets, and identify the metrics that actually drive decisions.
8. **Data-Driven Recommendation Synthesis** — "Given this analysis, what's your recommendation? What are the key risks?" Tests ability to synthesize multiple analytical threads into a clear, defensible strategic recommendation.

## Phase Structure
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Context & Problem Framing | 5 min | Business scenario presented. Candidate expected to ask clarifying questions about objectives, constraints, and available data. |
| Core Analytical Exercise | 15-20 min | Financial model, competitive analysis, or market sizing. May include provided dataset or exhibit. Interviewer probes assumptions and methodology throughout. |
| Quantitative Deep Dive | 10-12 min | Follow-up probes on model assumptions, sensitivity analysis, or alternative scenarios. Tests depth of understanding vs. surface-level execution. |
| Strategic Synthesis | 5-8 min | "So what?" — translate analysis into recommendation. Must address risks, tradeoffs, and implementation considerations. |
| Tool / Method Discussion | 5 min | Discussion of analytical tools, data sources, and methodologies used in past work. May include technical follow-ups on SQL, Python, or BI tools. |

## What Makes This Level Unique
- **Analysis must drive strategy, not just describe data.** "Revenue is declining" is observation. "Revenue decline is driven by 23% churn in mid-market segment due to competitor pricing — recommend value-based pricing restructure" is the bar.
- **Financial modeling depth increases significantly.** Expected to build and defend 3-statement models, DCF valuations, and scenario analyses. Must articulate assumption sensitivity — "A 200bp change in discount rate moves valuation by $15M."
- **Competitive analysis must be specific and current.** Generic SWOT fails. Must reference real competitors, market share data, recent moves, and strategic implications with quantitative backing.
- **Quantitative intuition matters.** "This market is roughly $2B growing at 5% annually" with rough reasoning — not just "it's large and growing."
- **Communication of analysis to non-technical stakeholders is tested.** Can you explain a DCF to a CEO who doesn't do finance? Can you summarize a complex dataset in 3 bullet points?
- **Firms test this differently:** McKinsey/Strategy& embed analytics in case interviews. Deloitte/Accenture may use standalone analytical exercises or case-lets with data exhibits. Corporate strategy roles often include take-home modeling exercises.

## Common Problems
- Build financial model for acquisition target (revenue, costs, synergies, IRR)
- Analyze customer cohort data to identify retention drivers
- Size a new market opportunity and estimate required investment
- Design pricing strategy based on competitive positioning and value delivered
- Evaluate whether to build, buy, or partner for market expansion
- Assess operational efficiency using benchmark comparison data
- Construct a strategic recommendation from multiple analytical inputs

## Anti-Patterns
- Reciting frameworks without adapting to context (generic Porter's without specific data)
- Building models without articulating key assumptions and sensitivities
- Analysis without "so what" — data description without strategic recommendation
- Ignoring competitive dynamics in market sizing
- Over-precision without acknowledging uncertainty ranges
- Single-metric myopia (optimizing one metric while ignoring tradeoffs)

## Probe Patterns
- "Which assumption in your model has the most impact on the outcome? How sensitive is the result?"
- "The CFO pushes back on your discount rate. How do you defend it?"
- "Your analysis shows two conflicting signals. How do you resolve the tension?"
- "Engineering says 6 months. Can you get 80% of the value in 6 weeks?"
- "How would your recommendation change if the competitive landscape shifts?"
- "Walk me through the first 30 days of implementing your recommendation."
