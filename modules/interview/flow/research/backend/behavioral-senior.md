# Backend Engineer — Behavioral Interview — Senior Level (7+ years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Career narrative and impact trajectory** — "Walk me through the biggest impact you've had in your career"
2. **Driving architecture changes across teams** — "Tell me about a time you drove a significant technical change that affected multiple teams"
3. **Leading without authority** — "Describe a time you influenced a decision when you had no formal authority"
4. **Navigating organizational ambiguity** — "Tell me about a time you had to make progress with conflicting requirements from different stakeholders"
5. **Building and mentoring teams** — "How have you invested in growing engineers on your team?"
6. **Strategic technical decision with business impact** — "Describe a technical decision that had significant business implications"
7. **Conflict resolution at organizational level** — "Tell me about a time senior leaders disagreed on a technical direction and how you helped resolve it"
8. **Setting engineering standards and culture** — "How have you established or changed engineering practices within your organization?"
9. **Risk anticipation and mitigation** — "Describe a time you identified a major risk before it became a problem"
10. **Failure with organizational consequences** — "Tell me about a significant failure and what you did differently afterward"
11. **Hiring and interview evaluation skills** — "How do you assess engineering talent? What do you look for when interviewing candidates?"

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (5 min):** Impact-trajectory narrative (NOT a resume walkthrough — a curated story about most significant contribution). Interviewer calibrates scope.
- **Leadership deep-dives (25-30 min):** 3-4 in-depth stories with extensive follow-up. Each story demonstrates multifaceted leadership across technical + people + process + strategic dimensions.
- **Organizational impact probe (10 min):** Culture-building, standards-setting, mentorship. Evidence of multiplicative impact (making the whole team better).
- **Closing (5 min):** Alignment on role scope, team dynamics, expectations for first 90 days.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Stories must demonstrate **multifaceted leadership across 4 dimensions**: (1) Technical leadership, (2) People leadership, (3) Process/operational leadership, (4) Strategic/business leadership.
- **Scope extends beyond immediate team** to domain or organization. Staff+ evaluated for impact on entire engineering org.
- **Persuasion and consensus-building** are core signals — influencing peers and managers through technical case-making.
- **3-5 year technical vision**: Must demonstrate forward-looking reasoning about technical direction.
- **Failure questions carry more weight** — interviewers want organizational learning and systemic process changes, not just personal growth.
- Behavioral interviews carry **significantly more weight** at Staff+ level and are often a deciding factor in hiring committee reviews. Meta hiring committees look directly at behavioral feedback for staff candidates.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Basic teamwork or "tell me about a time you worked with others" (too simple)
- Individual coding challenges or debugging stories (baseline, not differentiators)
- Questions answerable with a single project or limited scope
- Learning a new technology as primary topic (table stakes at this level)
- Questions that don't require demonstrating influence, only execution
- Anything an IC3/IC4 could answer equally well

## Probe Patterns
- **Deep follow-up:** Architecture-change stories (political landscape, who opposed, how consensus was built, what compromises), failure stories (organizational learning, not just personal), mentoring (specific systems built — pairing programs, knowledge-sharing initiatives)
- **Moderate depth:** Risk identification (detection method + mitigation plan), stakeholder conflict (both sides + resolution framework)
- **Surface treatment:** Self-introduction (brief — interviewer already knows background), closing questions
- **Unique probe:** Win-and-fail in the same skill area; "Tell me about a system you'd design differently today"
