# Backend Engineer — Behavioral Interview — Entry Level (0-2 years)

## Topic Sequence (typical order)
1. **Self-introduction / resume walkthrough** — "Tell me about yourself"
2. **Motivation and curiosity** — "Why backend engineering? What drew you to this role?"
3. **Learning a new technology** — "Tell me about a time you had to quickly learn a new programming language or technology"
4. **Team collaboration on a project** — "Tell me about a time you worked well within a team" (academic project, internship, or hackathon)
5. **Debugging a difficult problem** — "Describe a time you struggled with a coding problem and how you eventually resolved it"
6. **Handling feedback / constructive criticism** — "Tell me about a piece of critical feedback you received and how it helped you improve"
7. **Adaptability under pressure** — "Describe a project that didn't go as planned and how you adapted"
8. **Learning from failure** — "When did you make a mistake? How did you respond and what did you learn?"
9. **Communication with non-technical peers** — "How did you explain a technical decision to a non-technical teammate?"
10. **Closing: growth mindset** — "Where do you want to grow? What excites you about joining this team?"

## Phase Structure
- **Warm-up (5 min):** Resume walkthrough and motivation questions. Sets candidate at ease.
- **Core behavioral stories (20-25 min):** 3-4 STAR-format questions on teamwork, problem-solving, and learning. Interviewer probes with "What happened next?" Each answer runs 2-3 minutes.
- **Reflection probe (5 min):** Failure or mistake question, testing self-awareness and growth orientation.
- **Closing (5 min):** Candidate questions, role-fit signals, enthusiasm check.

## What Makes This Level Unique
- Stories come from **academic projects, internships, hackathons, and bootcamps** — not professional production systems. Interviewers explicitly accept non-professional context.
- Primary signal is **curiosity, enthusiasm, and willingness to learn** — not polished leadership narratives. Interviewers assess whether the candidate is "coachable."
- Emphasis on **basic collaboration** — can they work with others, take direction, participate in code reviews? Not leadership or cross-team influence.
- **Accountability without ownership scope** — asked about personal mistakes in limited-blast-radius settings.
- **Take-home projects** are a common alternative format for juniors — small API or feature build in 60-90 minutes.

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT ask at this level)
- Organizational strategy or technical direction-setting
- Cross-team influence or conflict resolution with senior stakeholders
- Mentoring / coaching others (they are the ones being mentored)
- Production incident management or on-call scenarios
- Multi-month project ownership or roadmap planning
- "How have you built engineering culture?"

## Probe Patterns
- **Deep follow-up:** Learning new tech (what resources, how fast, what blockers), debugging stories (actual technical approach), teamwork (specific role in the group)
- **Surface treatment:** Motivation questions, closing/growth, self-introduction
- **Classic probes:** "Is there something you wished you'd done differently?", "If you were in that situation today, would you handle it differently?"
