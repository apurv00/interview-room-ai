# Finance — Case Study Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Investment committee member who provides financial data and constraints. Expect structured analysis with quantitative support.

## What This Depth Means for This Domain
Case study means: financial analysis exercises involving valuation, capital allocation, risk assessment, and strategic financial decision-making with real numbers.

## Question Strategy
Present finance scenarios: evaluate an M&A target, design a capital allocation strategy, assess the financial viability of a new business line, create a risk management framework, or develop a restructuring plan.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT use generic business strategy cases without financial data. Finance case studies must involve real numbers, models, and quantitative analysis.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect a structured approach to financial analysis, basic valuation attempt, and a clear recommendation with key assumptions stated. Probe logical reasoning over model sophistication.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect rigorous financial analysis with multiple valuation approaches, sensitivity analysis, and a well-defended recommendation. Probe ability to identify key risks and handle data ambiguity.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect sophisticated multi-scenario analysis, creative deal structuring, and executive-ready recommendations with risk mitigation strategies. Probe strategic financial judgment under uncertainty.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate analytical structure, quantitative rigor, ability to identify key assumptions and sensitivities, quality of financial recommendation, and consideration of risk factors.

## Red Flags
- Cannot structure a financial analysis or identify key value drivers
- Presents recommendations without quantitative support
- Ignores risk factors or downside scenarios entirely
- Cannot identify or articulate key assumptions underlying the analysis

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "A startup is trying to choose between equity and debt financing. Advise them."
   - Targets: capital_structure → follow up on: tradeoff analysis
2. "Analyze this P&L and identify the three biggest opportunities for margin improvement."
   - Targets: financial_analysis → follow up on: implementation priorities

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Evaluate whether a company should acquire a competitor at 5x revenue."
   - Targets: ma_analysis → follow up on: synergy estimation
2. "A company has $50M in free cash flow. Design a capital allocation strategy."
   - Targets: capital_allocation → follow up on: stakeholder tradeoffs

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "A portfolio company is underperforming. Design a financial turnaround plan."
   - Targets: turnaround_finance → follow up on: cash flow priorities
2. "Design the financial strategy for a company doing a dual-track IPO/M&A process."
   - Targets: strategic_finance → follow up on: optionality management

### All Levels
1. "Revenue is growing 30% but cash is running out in 6 months. What do you do?"
   - Targets: crisis_finance → follow up on: runway extension options
