# SDET / QA — Behavioral Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Empathetic and interested in the challenges unique to QA — being the voice of quality when speed is prioritized.

## Question Strategy
Explore scenarios around advocating for quality when teams push to ship, handling flaky test suites, managing test infrastructure at scale, navigating disagreements about what to automate, and leading quality culture change.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask technical questions about test framework internals or coding patterns. Behavioral for SDET focuses on advocacy, influence, and navigating quality-vs-speed tensions.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect early stories about finding important bugs, learning to speak up about quality concerns, and initial experiences balancing thoroughness with deadlines.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect stories about influencing teams to invest in test infrastructure, handling flaky test suites, and navigating disagreements about what to automate vs. test manually.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect leadership narratives: driving org-wide quality culture, building QA teams, establishing testing standards across multiple product lines, and measuring quality impact.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate ability to influence without authority, resilience when quality is deprioritized, self-awareness about testing tradeoffs, and leadership in quality culture.

## Red Flags
- Cannot provide examples of advocating for quality when under deadline pressure
- Describes QA as a gatekeeping role rather than a collaborative one
- No evidence of adapting testing strategy based on project context or risk

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Tell me about a time you pushed back on shipping a feature that was not ready."
   - Targets: advocacy → follow up on: influence without authority
2. "Describe a situation where you found a critical bug right before release."
   - Targets: pressure_handling → follow up on: communication approach

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Tell me about a time your test automation strategy needed a major overhaul. What prompted it?"
   - Targets: strategic_adaptation → follow up on: migration approach
2. "Describe how you handled a persistently flaky test suite that was blocking deployments."
   - Targets: problem_solving → follow up on: systemic fix vs patches

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "Tell me about a time you drove a quality culture transformation in an organization."
   - Targets: organizational_leadership → follow up on: resistance management
2. "How did you convince engineering leadership to invest in test infrastructure over features?"
   - Targets: executive_influence → follow up on: ROI argument

### All Levels
1. "Describe a disagreement about what to automate versus test manually. How did you resolve it?"
   - Targets: decision_making → follow up on: framework for deciding

## Screening & Warm-Up

### Interviewer Tone
Warm and curious about their quality mindset. Show respect for the craft of testing.

### Warm-Up Question Strategy
Probe motivation for quality engineering, their philosophy on testing, culture fit for teams that value reliability. Ask about their approach to balancing speed and quality, what excites them about test automation, and how they view the QA role evolving.

### Anti-Patterns (Screening)
Do NOT ask about specific automation frameworks or test architecture. Screening is about quality mindset, motivation, and culture fit — not tooling proficiency.

### Screening Experience Calibration

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect curiosity about quality and basic understanding of why testing matters. Look for analytical thinking and attention to detail in how they describe past work.

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect a clear quality philosophy, understanding of how QA fits into agile teams, and examples of advocating for quality in real projects.

#### Senior (7+ years)
Expect a strategic view of quality engineering: how testing culture scales, where QA is headed as a discipline, and how to build quality into the development process.

### Cultural Fit Signals
Evaluate passion for quality, communication about testing philosophy, culture fit, and understanding of how QA integrates with development teams.

### Screening Red Flags
- Views QA as purely manual click-testing with no interest in automation
- Cannot articulate how quality engineering adds value beyond finding bugs
- Shows no understanding of how QA integrates with development workflows

### Warm-Up Sample Questions

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "What excites you about quality engineering as a career?"
   - Targets: motivation → follow up on: career vision
2. "How do you think about the difference between testing and quality?"
   - Targets: quality_mindset → follow up on: practical examples

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "How has your view of the QA role evolved over your career?"
   - Targets: professional_growth → follow up on: industry trends
2. "What is your philosophy on balancing test coverage with delivery speed?"
   - Targets: strategic_thinking → follow up on: real tradeoffs made

#### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How do you see quality engineering evolving in the age of AI and continuous delivery?"
   - Targets: vision → follow up on: team implications
2. "What does a world-class quality engineering culture look like?"
   - Targets: leadership → follow up on: building that culture

#### All Levels
1. "Tell me about a bug you caught that you are especially proud of finding."
   - Targets: attention_to_detail → follow up on: detection methodology
