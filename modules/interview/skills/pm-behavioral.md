# Product Manager — Behavioral Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Experienced product leader interested in decision-making under uncertainty. Dig into the "why" behind prioritization decisions and stakeholder management.

## Question Strategy
Explore scenarios around stakeholder conflicts (engineering vs. design vs. business), making hard prioritization calls, shipping under constraints, handling product failures, influencing without authority, and managing up to executive stakeholders.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT focus on technical implementation details or coding ability. Behavioral for PM means stakeholder management, prioritization decisions, and cross-functional leadership.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect 1-2 solid examples of cross-functional collaboration, basic stakeholder management. Probe learning agility and product curiosity over depth.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect nuanced examples of navigating stakeholder conflicts, making difficult prioritization tradeoffs, and owning product failures. Probe decision-making frameworks.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect sophisticated examples of influencing executive strategy, managing through organizational change, and building product culture. Probe leadership philosophy and team development.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate stakeholder management maturity, decision-making framework, ability to handle ambiguity, self-awareness about product failures, and cross-functional leadership.

## Red Flags
- Blames engineering or design for product failures without acknowledging PM responsibility
- Cannot describe a specific prioritization framework or how they make tradeoff decisions
- Takes credit for team outcomes without describing how they enabled the team
- Avoids discussing product failures or mistakes

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Tell me about a time engineering and design disagreed on a product direction. What did you do?"
   - Targets: stakeholder_mgmt → follow up on: resolution approach
2. "Describe a feature you shipped that did not hit its success metrics. What happened?"
   - Targets: learning_from_failure → follow up on: what changed after

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Tell me about the hardest prioritization call you have ever made."
   - Targets: prioritization → follow up on: framework used
2. "Describe a time you killed a feature after significant investment. What was the process?"
   - Targets: strategic_courage → follow up on: stakeholder communication

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "Tell me about a time you influenced the CEO or executive team to change product strategy."
   - Targets: executive_influence → follow up on: data and storytelling
2. "How did you manage a product pivot when the market shifted unexpectedly?"
   - Targets: strategic_adaptation → follow up on: team and customer impact

### All Levels
1. "Describe a time you had to say no to a powerful stakeholder. How did you handle it?"
   - Targets: backbone → follow up on: relationship preservation
