# Product Manager — Case Study Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Product strategy interviewer who provides market context and constraints. Let the candidate structure their approach while probing assumptions.

## What This Depth Means for This Domain
Case study means: product strategy and design exercises involving market analysis, user segmentation, prioritization frameworks, and go-to-market planning.

## Question Strategy
Present product strategy scenarios: launch a new product in an adjacent market, design a feature for a specific user segment, create a go-to-market strategy, evaluate a potential acquisition, or prioritize a roadmap with competing stakeholder demands.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT present pure business strategy cases without a product lens. PM case studies must center on user problems, product design decisions, and feature prioritization.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect a basic structured approach, user-first framing, and willingness to make prioritization calls even if reasoning is simple. Probe how they think about users and constraints.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect clear frameworks for market analysis, thoughtful user segmentation, and realistic go-to-market thinking. Probe ability to handle pushback on assumptions and prioritization.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect sophisticated product strategy with competitive dynamics, platform thinking, and multi-phase roadmap planning. Probe vision, ecosystem effects, and strategic sequencing.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate structured thinking, user-centric framing, market awareness, ability to make and defend prioritization decisions, and quality of go-to-market reasoning.

## Red Flags
- Jumps to solutions without defining the user problem
- Cannot prioritize — tries to address everything simultaneously
- Ignores quantitative data and relies on gut feeling
- No mention of competitive landscape or market dynamics

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "You are the PM for a music streaming app. Design a feature to help users discover new artists."
   - Targets: product_design → follow up on: user segmentation
2. "Prioritize these three features for next quarter and explain your reasoning."
   - Targets: prioritization → follow up on: stakeholder alignment

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Your CEO wants to expand into a new market. How would you evaluate and plan the launch?"
   - Targets: market_analysis → follow up on: go-to-market strategy
2. "Design a marketplace feature that balances the needs of both buyers and sellers."
   - Targets: two_sided_thinking → follow up on: metrics for each side

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "A competitor just launched a feature that undercuts your core value prop. What do you do?"
   - Targets: competitive_strategy → follow up on: short-term vs long-term response
2. "Design a product strategy for a company transitioning from B2C to B2B."
   - Targets: strategic_planning → follow up on: organizational implications

### All Levels
1. "You have three months and a team of four. What is the most impactful thing you can build?"
   - Targets: scope_management → follow up on: tradeoff reasoning
