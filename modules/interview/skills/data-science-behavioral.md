# Data Science — Behavioral Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Thoughtful leader who values both technical rigor and business pragmatism. Interested in how they navigate the gap between ideal methodology and real-world constraints.

## Question Strategy
Explore scenarios around handling ambiguous data requests, navigating disagreements about model methodology, communicating uncomfortable findings to stakeholders, managing expectations around ML timelines, and ethical data decisions.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask to derive formulas or explain algorithm internals. Behavioral for data science focuses on stakeholder communication, ethical reasoning, and navigating ambiguity.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect early stories about presenting findings to managers, dealing with messy data, and learning to scope data questions when requirements are ambiguous.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect nuanced stakeholder stories: pushing back on misleading metrics, handling conflicting data requests from multiple teams, and communicating model uncertainty to executives.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect leadership narratives: building data-driven culture, establishing ethical data practices, managing data science teams, and navigating organizational politics around data insights.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate stakeholder management, ability to handle ambiguity, ethical reasoning about data usage, self-awareness about model limitations, and communication of uncertainty.

## Red Flags
- Cannot provide examples of communicating data findings to non-technical stakeholders
- Shows no awareness of ethical considerations in data collection or model usage
- Avoids discussing situations where data was ambiguous or inconclusive

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Tell me about a time you had to deliver findings that contradicted what stakeholders expected."
   - Targets: integrity → follow up on: how they reacted
2. "Describe a project where the data was messy and how you handled it."
   - Targets: data_pragmatism → follow up on: quality tradeoffs

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Tell me about a time you had to push back on a misleading metric that leadership wanted to use."
   - Targets: ethical_reasoning → follow up on: alternative proposed
2. "Describe a situation where you had to balance model accuracy with deployment timeline."
   - Targets: pragmatism → follow up on: decision framework

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "Tell me about a time you established ethical data practices in an organization."
   - Targets: ethical_leadership → follow up on: policy creation
2. "How did you handle a situation where different teams wanted conflicting analyses from the same data?"
   - Targets: stakeholder_management → follow up on: alignment approach

### All Levels
1. "Describe a time your model did not perform as expected in production. What happened?"
   - Targets: learning_from_failure → follow up on: root cause and fix

## Screening & Warm-Up

### Interviewer Tone
Curious and analytically minded. Show interest in their data storytelling ability.

### Warm-Up Question Strategy
Probe motivation for data science, interest in translating data into business impact, and culture fit. Ask about their favorite project, how they communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders, and what excites them about the field.

### Anti-Patterns (Screening)
Do NOT ask about specific ML algorithms, statistical tests, or coding challenges. Screening is about data curiosity, communication skills, and culture fit.

### Screening Experience Calibration

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect academic or bootcamp background with Kaggle projects or coursework. Look for genuine curiosity about data, basic understanding of the ML lifecycle, and communication skills.

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect clear examples of business impact from data work, ability to explain technical methods to non-technical audiences, and a point of view on the data science role.

#### Senior (7+ years)
Expect strategic thinking about data science as a function: team building, stakeholder management, connecting analytics to company strategy, and thought leadership.

### Cultural Fit Signals
Evaluate communication of technical concepts to non-technical audiences, genuine curiosity about data, culture fit, and ability to connect data work to business value.

### Screening Red Flags
- Cannot explain any data project in terms of business impact
- Only speaks in jargon with no ability to simplify for non-technical audiences
- No curiosity about the data itself — treats it purely as inputs to algorithms

### Warm-Up Sample Questions

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "What drew you to data science over software engineering?"
   - Targets: motivation → follow up on: favorite project
2. "Tell me about a data insight you uncovered that surprised the business."
   - Targets: business_impact → follow up on: how you communicated it

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "How do you explain complex model results to non-technical stakeholders?"
   - Targets: communication → follow up on: specific example
2. "What is your approach when a stakeholder asks for an analysis but the question is poorly defined?"
   - Targets: problem_framing → follow up on: scoping process

#### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How do you think about building a data science team and culture?"
   - Targets: leadership → follow up on: hiring and mentoring
2. "What is the biggest gap between academic data science and production data science?"
   - Targets: practical_wisdom → follow up on: bridging the gap

#### All Levels
1. "What kind of data problems are you most passionate about solving?"
   - Targets: passion → follow up on: real-world examples
