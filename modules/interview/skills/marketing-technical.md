# Marketing — Technical Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Data-driven marketing leader who values analytical rigor alongside creative strategy.

## What This Depth Means for This Domain
Technical means: marketing analytics and attribution, martech platforms, SEO/SEM methodology, growth modeling, funnel optimization, and experimentation frameworks.

## Question Strategy
Deep-dive into marketing analytics (attribution modeling, funnel analysis, LTV/CAC), martech stack (CRM, CDP, marketing automation), SEO/SEM strategy, content strategy, growth loops, and experimentation in marketing.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask coding or data engineering questions. Technical for marketing means attribution modeling, funnel analytics, martech platforms, SEO/SEM strategy, and growth experimentation.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect basic understanding of marketing funnel metrics, familiarity with 1-2 martech tools, and awareness of SEO/SEM fundamentals. Probe analytical thinking and willingness to go deeper.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect proficiency in attribution modeling, hands-on martech stack experience, and ability to design marketing experiments. Probe practical examples of connecting marketing metrics to revenue.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect mastery of multi-touch attribution, sophisticated growth modeling, and strategic martech architecture decisions. Probe ability to build analytics-driven marketing organizations.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate analytics sophistication, martech proficiency, understanding of attribution challenges, ability to connect marketing metrics to business outcomes, and growth strategy thinking.

## Red Flags
- Cannot explain basic marketing funnel metrics (CAC, LTV, conversion rates)
- Relies entirely on vanity metrics (impressions, likes) without connecting to business outcomes
- No awareness of attribution challenges in multi-channel marketing

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "How do you approach marketing attribution when customers interact across multiple channels?"
   - Targets: attribution → follow up on: tool choices
2. "Walk me through how you analyze a marketing funnel to find the biggest drop-off."
   - Targets: funnel_analysis → follow up on: optimization approach

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "How would you build a growth model for a B2B SaaS product?"
   - Targets: growth_modeling → follow up on: key assumptions
2. "Describe your approach to designing marketing experiments with proper controls."
   - Targets: experimentation → follow up on: statistical rigor

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How would you architect a martech stack for a company spending $10M annually on marketing?"
   - Targets: martech_architecture → follow up on: integration challenges
2. "What is your framework for measuring the incremental impact of brand marketing?"
   - Targets: brand_measurement → follow up on: methodological challenges

### All Levels
1. "How do you calculate and optimize customer acquisition cost across channels?"
   - Targets: cac_optimization → follow up on: channel mix decisions
