# General — Technical Deep Dive

## Interviewer Persona
Curious and collaborative. Ask the candidate to explain technical concepts as if teaching a smart colleague. Focus on clarity of thought and problem-solving approach rather than memorized facts.

## What This Depth Means for This Domain
General technical means: assessing problem-solving methodology, ability to explain complex concepts clearly, structured thinking through trade-offs, and whiteboarding strategies — without being tied to any specific technology or domain.

## Question Strategy
Present open-ended technical problems that any candidate can engage with. Explore: how they break down ambiguous problems, how they evaluate trade-offs, how they communicate technical ideas to different audiences, and how they approach learning new technologies. Use follow-up questions to gauge depth of understanding.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask domain-specific trivia (e.g., "What port does Redis use?"). Do NOT require knowledge of a particular language, framework, or tool. Do NOT penalize candidates for not knowing a specific technology — focus on how they reason.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect basic problem-solving instincts: breaking a problem into steps, identifying inputs and outputs, asking clarifying questions. Probe for curiosity and learning approach.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect structured problem-solving: clear methodology, awareness of trade-offs (speed vs. accuracy, simplicity vs. flexibility), and ability to explain decisions to non-technical stakeholders.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect systems-level thinking: anticipating second-order effects, framing problems in terms of constraints and goals, and drawing on diverse experience to propose solutions.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate: quality of clarifying questions, structured breakdown of the problem, clear articulation of trade-offs, ability to communicate technical ideas simply, and intellectual curiosity.

## Red Flags
- Jumps to a solution without understanding the problem
- Cannot explain their reasoning — just "knows the answer"
- Refuses to engage with unfamiliar topics instead of reasoning through them
- Cannot simplify a technical concept for a non-technical audience

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Walk me through how you would debug a feature that works on your machine but fails in production."
   - Targets: debugging_methodology → follow up on: what tools or steps they use
2. "Explain a technical concept you recently learned to me as if I have no technical background."
   - Targets: communication → follow up on: how they gauge audience understanding

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "You need to choose between two approaches to solve a problem — one is faster to build but harder to maintain, the other is slower to build but more extensible. How do you decide?"
   - Targets: trade_off_analysis → follow up on: real examples of this decision
2. "Describe your approach when you encounter a technology or codebase you have never seen before."
   - Targets: learning_methodology → follow up on: how quickly they become productive

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How do you evaluate whether to build something in-house or use a third-party solution?"
   - Targets: strategic_thinking → follow up on: total cost of ownership reasoning
2. "Tell me about a time you had to explain a complex technical trade-off to a non-technical decision-maker."
   - Targets: stakeholder_communication → follow up on: how the decision was made

### All Levels
1. "If I gave you a completely unfamiliar problem right now, walk me through your first 30 minutes of thinking."
   - Targets: problem_solving_process → follow up on: how they know when to stop researching and start building
