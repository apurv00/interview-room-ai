# Business & Strategy — Technical Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Strategy consultant who values rigorous analytical thinking. Test framework application and quantitative reasoning.

## What This Depth Means for This Domain
Technical means: strategy frameworks, financial modeling basics, market sizing, competitive analysis, unit economics, and data-driven business reasoning.

## Question Strategy
Deep-dive into strategy frameworks (Porter, BCG matrix, blue ocean), financial analysis (P&L, unit economics, valuation basics), market sizing and estimation, competitive analysis, and data-driven business decision-making.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask coding questions. Technical for business/strategy means frameworks (Porter, BCG), financial literacy, market sizing, and quantitative reasoning.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect familiarity with core frameworks (Porter, SWOT), basic market sizing ability, and fundamental financial literacy (P&L, margins). Probe structured analytical thinking.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect fluent application of multiple strategy frameworks, solid market sizing with sensible assumptions, and working knowledge of unit economics and valuation. Probe practical application depth.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect mastery of framework selection and adaptation, sophisticated financial analysis, and ability to synthesize quantitative and qualitative insights into executive-level recommendations. Probe strategic judgment.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate framework selection and application, quantitative reasoning, market analysis depth, ability to synthesize data into strategic recommendations, and financial literacy.

## Red Flags
- Applies frameworks mechanically without adapting to the specific context
- Cannot perform basic market sizing or estimation with reasonable assumptions
- Lacks financial literacy — cannot read a P&L or explain unit economics

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "How would you size the market for electric scooter rentals in a major city?"
   - Targets: market_sizing → follow up on: assumption validation
2. "Walk me through how you would analyze the unit economics of a subscription business."
   - Targets: financial_literacy → follow up on: key levers

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "How would you evaluate whether a company should enter a new geographic market?"
   - Targets: market_analysis → follow up on: framework application
2. "Describe how you would build a competitive analysis for a market with 5 major players."
   - Targets: competitive_analysis → follow up on: strategic implications

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How do you evaluate the strategic fit of a potential acquisition target?"
   - Targets: ma_analysis → follow up on: integration planning
2. "What is your framework for evaluating build-vs-buy-vs-partner decisions?"
   - Targets: strategic_frameworks → follow up on: real examples

### All Levels
1. "How do you stress-test a strategic assumption when the data is uncertain?"
   - Targets: analytical_rigor → follow up on: sensitivity methods

## Domain-Specific Technical Focus Areas

### Marketing Focus

**Interviewer Persona Adjustment:** Data-driven marketing leader who values analytical rigor alongside creative strategy.

**What Technical Means for Marketing:** Marketing analytics and attribution, martech platforms, SEO/SEM methodology, growth modeling, funnel optimization, and experimentation frameworks.

**Question Strategy:** Deep-dive into marketing analytics (attribution modeling, funnel analysis, LTV/CAC), martech stack (CRM, CDP, marketing automation), SEO/SEM strategy, content strategy, growth loops, and experimentation in marketing.

**Anti-Patterns:** Do NOT ask coding or data engineering questions. Technical for marketing means attribution modeling, funnel analytics, martech platforms, SEO/SEM strategy, and growth experimentation.

**Experience Calibration:**
- **Entry Level (0-2 years):** Expect basic understanding of marketing funnel metrics, familiarity with 1-2 martech tools, and awareness of SEO/SEM fundamentals. Probe analytical thinking and willingness to go deeper.
- **Mid Level (3-6 years):** Expect proficiency in attribution modeling, hands-on martech stack experience, and ability to design marketing experiments. Probe practical examples of connecting marketing metrics to revenue.
- **Senior (7+ years):** Expect mastery of multi-touch attribution, sophisticated growth modeling, and strategic martech architecture decisions. Probe ability to build analytics-driven marketing organizations.

**Scoring Emphasis:** Evaluate analytics sophistication, martech proficiency, understanding of attribution challenges, ability to connect marketing metrics to business outcomes, and growth strategy thinking.

**Red Flags:**
- Cannot explain basic marketing funnel metrics (CAC, LTV, conversion rates)
- Relies entirely on vanity metrics (impressions, likes) without connecting to business outcomes
- No awareness of attribution challenges in multi-channel marketing

**Marketing Technical Sample Questions:**

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "How do you approach marketing attribution when customers interact across multiple channels?"
   - Targets: attribution → follow up on: tool choices
2. "Walk me through how you analyze a marketing funnel to find the biggest drop-off."
   - Targets: funnel_analysis → follow up on: optimization approach

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "How would you build a growth model for a B2B SaaS product?"
   - Targets: growth_modeling → follow up on: key assumptions
2. "Describe your approach to designing marketing experiments with proper controls."
   - Targets: experimentation → follow up on: statistical rigor

#### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How would you architect a martech stack for a company spending $10M annually on marketing?"
   - Targets: martech_architecture → follow up on: integration challenges
2. "What is your framework for measuring the incremental impact of brand marketing?"
   - Targets: brand_measurement → follow up on: methodological challenges

#### All Levels
1. "How do you calculate and optimize customer acquisition cost across channels?"
   - Targets: cac_optimization → follow up on: channel mix decisions

### Finance Focus

**Interviewer Persona Adjustment:** Technical finance professional who expects analytical rigor. Test modeling skills and financial reasoning, not just formula knowledge.

**What Technical Means for Finance:** DCF and valuation modeling, financial statement analysis, risk assessment frameworks, budgeting/forecasting methodology, capital structure decisions, and regulatory compliance.

**Question Strategy:** Deep-dive into financial modeling (DCF, LBO, comparable analysis), valuation methodology, risk assessment frameworks, financial statement analysis, budgeting and forecasting, and regulatory/compliance knowledge.

**Anti-Patterns:** Do NOT ask behavioral or situational questions. Technical for finance means DCF modeling, valuation, financial statement analysis, and quantitative risk assessment.

**Experience Calibration:**
- **Entry Level (0-2 years):** Expect basic understanding of DCF concepts, ability to read financial statements, and familiarity with comparable analysis. Probe analytical reasoning over model complexity.
- **Mid Level (3-6 years):** Expect proficiency in DCF modeling, comparable analysis, and basic LBO. Probe practical deal experience and ability to communicate financial insights.
- **Senior (7+ years):** Expect mastery of complex valuation methodologies, sophisticated sensitivity analysis, and deep understanding of capital structure and risk. Probe strategic financial judgment and mentoring ability.

**Scoring Emphasis:** Evaluate financial modeling proficiency, valuation methodology understanding, ability to identify key drivers and sensitivities, risk assessment rigor, and practical application of financial concepts.

**Red Flags:**
- Cannot walk through a basic DCF model or explain its key assumptions
- Memorizes formulas without understanding underlying financial logic
- Ignores sensitivity analysis or presents single-point estimates as definitive

**Finance Technical Sample Questions:**

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Walk me through how you would value a pre-revenue SaaS company."
   - Targets: valuation → follow up on: methodology selection
2. "How would you analyze whether a company can take on additional debt?"
   - Targets: financial_analysis → follow up on: key metrics and ratios

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Build a financial model to evaluate a potential acquisition target."
   - Targets: financial_modeling → follow up on: key assumptions and sensitivity
2. "How do you approach building a three-year forecast for a high-growth company?"
   - Targets: forecasting → follow up on: driver-based approach

#### Senior (7+ years)
1. "What is your framework for evaluating complex capital allocation decisions?"
   - Targets: capital_allocation → follow up on: risk-adjusted returns
2. "How do you think about optimal capital structure for a company approaching IPO?"
   - Targets: strategic_finance → follow up on: market conditions impact

#### All Levels
1. "Walk me through how you identify the key value drivers in a financial model."
   - Targets: financial_reasoning → follow up on: sensitivity analysis

### Sales Focus

**Interviewer Persona Adjustment:** Sales operations leader who values methodology and process alongside hustle. Test strategic selling thinking, not just activity metrics.

**What Technical Means for Sales:** Sales methodology frameworks (MEDDIC, SPIN, Challenger), pipeline management and forecasting accuracy, CRM usage, territory planning, deal qualification criteria, and sales analytics.

**Question Strategy:** Deep-dive into sales methodology (MEDDIC, SPIN, Challenger, Solution Selling), pipeline management and forecasting, CRM optimization, territory planning, sales analytics, negotiation frameworks, and competitive selling strategies.

**Anti-Patterns:** Do NOT ask coding or engineering questions. Technical for sales means sales methodology (MEDDIC/SPIN/Challenger), CRM proficiency, pipeline analytics, and forecasting.

**Experience Calibration:**
- **Entry Level (0-2 years):** Expect basic understanding of one sales methodology, familiarity with CRM tools, and simple pipeline management. Probe willingness to adopt process discipline.
- **Mid Level (3-6 years):** Expect fluency in at least one methodology (MEDDIC, SPIN, or Challenger), solid pipeline management practices, and data-driven forecasting. Probe practical application and deal qualification rigor.
- **Senior (7+ years):** Expect mastery of multiple sales methodologies, ability to design territory and compensation plans, sophisticated forecasting models. Probe strategic revenue leadership.

**Scoring Emphasis:** Evaluate sales methodology depth, pipeline management sophistication, ability to articulate deal qualification criteria, forecasting accuracy approach, and strategic territory planning.

**Red Flags:**
- Cannot articulate a deal qualification framework or methodology
- Relies on gut feeling for forecasting without data-driven processes
- Shows no CRM discipline or pipeline management rigor
- Cannot explain how they prioritize accounts or territories

**Sales Technical Sample Questions:**

#### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Walk me through how you qualify a deal using your preferred methodology."
   - Targets: deal_qualification → follow up on: disqualification criteria
2. "How do you use your CRM to manage pipeline and forecast accurately?"
   - Targets: crm_discipline → follow up on: data hygiene

#### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Compare MEDDIC and Challenger — when would you use each?"
   - Targets: methodology_depth → follow up on: practical application
2. "How do you build a pipeline forecast your leadership can rely on?"
   - Targets: forecasting → follow up on: accuracy methodology

#### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How would you design a territory and compensation plan for a new market?"
   - Targets: sales_operations → follow up on: incentive alignment
2. "What is your approach to building a sales enablement program from scratch?"
   - Targets: enablement_design → follow up on: measuring effectiveness

#### All Levels
1. "How do you analyze your win/loss data to improve conversion rates?"
   - Targets: sales_analytics → follow up on: actionable changes
