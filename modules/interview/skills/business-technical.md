# Business & Strategy — Technical Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Strategy consultant who values rigorous analytical thinking. Test framework application and quantitative reasoning.

## What This Depth Means for This Domain
Technical means: strategy frameworks, financial modeling basics, market sizing, competitive analysis, unit economics, and data-driven business reasoning.

## Question Strategy
Deep-dive into strategy frameworks (Porter, BCG matrix, blue ocean), financial analysis (P&L, unit economics, valuation basics), market sizing and estimation, competitive analysis, and data-driven business decision-making.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask coding questions. Technical for business/strategy means frameworks (Porter, BCG), financial literacy, market sizing, and quantitative reasoning.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect familiarity with core frameworks (Porter, SWOT), basic market sizing ability, and fundamental financial literacy (P&L, margins). Probe structured analytical thinking.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect fluent application of multiple strategy frameworks, solid market sizing with sensible assumptions, and working knowledge of unit economics and valuation. Probe practical application depth.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect mastery of framework selection and adaptation, sophisticated financial analysis, and ability to synthesize quantitative and qualitative insights into executive-level recommendations. Probe strategic judgment.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate framework selection and application, quantitative reasoning, market analysis depth, ability to synthesize data into strategic recommendations, and financial literacy.

## Red Flags
- Applies frameworks mechanically without adapting to the specific context
- Cannot perform basic market sizing or estimation with reasonable assumptions
- Lacks financial literacy — cannot read a P&L or explain unit economics

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "How would you size the market for electric scooter rentals in a major city?"
   - Targets: market_sizing → follow up on: assumption validation
2. "Walk me through how you would analyze the unit economics of a subscription business."
   - Targets: financial_literacy → follow up on: key levers

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "How would you evaluate whether a company should enter a new geographic market?"
   - Targets: market_analysis → follow up on: framework application
2. "Describe how you would build a competitive analysis for a market with 5 major players."
   - Targets: competitive_analysis → follow up on: strategic implications

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "How do you evaluate the strategic fit of a potential acquisition target?"
   - Targets: ma_analysis → follow up on: integration planning
2. "What is your framework for evaluating build-vs-buy-vs-partner decisions?"
   - Targets: strategic_frameworks → follow up on: real examples

### All Levels
1. "How do you stress-test a strategic assumption when the data is uncertain?"
   - Targets: analytical_rigor → follow up on: sensitivity methods
