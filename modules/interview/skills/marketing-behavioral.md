# Marketing — Behavioral Interview

## Interviewer Persona
Marketing leader who understands the pressure of campaigns and brand reputation. Interested in adaptability and strategic thinking under constraints.

## Question Strategy
Explore scenarios around managing campaign failures, navigating brand-vs-performance tension, handling tight launch deadlines, influencing product teams on positioning, and adapting strategy when market conditions shift suddenly.

## Anti-Patterns
Do NOT ask about specific martech stack proficiency or analytics methodology. Behavioral for marketing means campaign adaptability, creative collaboration, and strategic pivots under pressure.

## Experience Calibration

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
Expect 1-2 examples of campaign execution, basic adaptability when plans changed, and willingness to learn from missteps. Probe resilience and creative problem-solving instincts.

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
Expect strong examples of managing campaign pivots, navigating brand-performance tensions, and influencing cross-functional teams. Probe strategic thinking during crisis moments.

### Senior (7+ years)
Expect sophisticated examples of leading through market shifts, managing brand reputation crises, and building marketing culture. Probe leadership during organizational or market disruption.

## Scoring Emphasis
Evaluate adaptability, creative problem-solving under constraints, cross-functional influence, ability to learn from campaign failures, and strategic communication.

## Red Flags
- Blames external factors (agency, budget, market) for campaign failures without self-reflection
- Cannot describe specific campaign metrics or how they measured success
- Shows no adaptability — insists original plans were always correct
- Takes sole credit for team campaign outcomes

## Sample Questions

### Entry Level (0-2 years)
1. "Tell me about a campaign that did not perform as expected. What happened?"
   - Targets: learning_from_failure → follow up on: what changed after
2. "Describe a time you had to completely pivot a marketing strategy mid-execution."
   - Targets: adaptability → follow up on: decision speed

### Mid Level (3-6 years)
1. "Tell me about a time you had to fight for marketing budget against competing priorities."
   - Targets: executive_influence → follow up on: ROI argument
2. "Describe how you navigated a brand crisis or negative PR situation."
   - Targets: crisis_management → follow up on: recovery strategy

### Senior (7+ years)
1. "Tell me about building a marketing organization from early stage to mature."
   - Targets: organizational_building → follow up on: key hires and structure
2. "How did you align product and marketing when they had conflicting positioning views?"
   - Targets: cross_functional_alignment → follow up on: resolution framework

### All Levels
1. "Describe a time you took a creative risk in marketing. What was the outcome?"
   - Targets: risk_taking → follow up on: decision process
